Elk Mound, WI

2003 - p8.

Wisconsin Deer Season

November 22 - The WI Opener

Finally, Phil looks off to the left and finds the deer dead by a tree as it tried to go up over the bank towards the creek.  It only bled for the last few yards before it dropped.  The deer turns out to be a buck fawn, shot at about 2 p.m.  Phil goes to get Lorin so we can tag it with an antler less deer tag.  After a quick gut job I drag it back out to the creek and across the bridge so we can pick it up later with Phil's truck.  

We make a drive or two more in rifle country and then call it for the day.  Total take on opening day - two bucks and three antler less deer.  

November 23 - Day 2

The morning is a bit cooler and less overcast, so it is easier to see but less comfortable for sitting.  Everyone sits back at their stands but report no deer movement at mid-morning.  So we gather back at the vehicles and decide that we will go over to shotgun hunting and make some drives there.  Once the hunting gang meets up at shotgun we get out and begin uncasing our shotguns.  All of a sudden what started out as a light rain turned into very heavy rain/sleet.  We decide that the deer won't be moving in this weather and that we would only get wet so we call it a day.  Lorin and I go back to the hotel and watch the weather report which shows a huge snow storm moving in the area.  I get a call from Cathy that I'm expected back at home in the twin cities so I pack up and drive back through the snow which by this time is blowing pretty hard.  There is also a few inches already on the ground in the cities.  Fortunately the drive back home is a harried, but safe one.  So that ends the 2003 season for Eric.  

Gene's opening day, again, was pretty interesting.
