Drummond Wisconsin

1992 -  p.2.

Year 3 Bayfield County

Sat. 3/8 - Post-Season Scouting

Behind Eric's stand and 50 yards North of Gene's stand I found a trail hub where several deeply worn trails intersect.  It looks promising for a stand site so I drop a 50-pound block of salt.  Then, heading West of Gene's stand we find another trail hub where we drop a second block of salt.  Before Allen comes back I head West to the creek from the shack and then turn North for a distance.  I see a doe moving South along the creek but she doesn't see me.  I continue on and hit a logging trail and follow it East back to the main logging road that runs behind the shack.

Sun. 3/8 - Post-Season Scouting

Eric heads back to Minneapolis in the a.m. to attend the MN Deer Classic show at the state fairgrounds.  Gene and Allen went to Porcupine Lake to go ice fishing.  Gene gets a bite the first four times down the hole.  Not many fish are caught so they leave for Wausau in the p.m.

Oct. 23  - Pre-Season Scouting

I have really gotten into topographic maps as a scouting tool.  One of the "big-buck" hotspots I have been reading about is to find a "swamp island", a hideaway near a mainland that bucks escape to when the hunting pressure gets too high.  I find what I think is an ideal setup that is across from the area we hunted last fall.  It looks to be about one-half to a one-mile walk back in there.  The woods are initially fairly open and then get thick as I cross a swamp between the road and the swamp island.  Once on the swamp island, I find a ridge off to the left and follow it East down to the point where the swamp circles around and separates the island from the mainland.  I found some old buck rubs on a trail and a few small scrapes.  Nearby, I find a big pine tree that I can hang my portable tree stand in.  Once I was done with that, I dropped a small mineral and salt block down nearby and then hike back out of the woods.  On the way out I put "bright eyes" tacks on the trees to mark the stand trail.  I figure it will take me about 45 minutes to walk in on opening morning.  

When I got out to the car I looked some more at the topo map and pick out two more places to scout.  One looks very promising with about 6 scrapes and 9 buck rubs along a logging trail, but it is also very accessible from the road.  Based on the number of signs pointing to cabins and resorts in the area I abandon it as a site that probably would get too much hunting pressure on opening day. 

Gene arrives at the shack about 4 p.m.  We continue to put Styrofoam up in the ceiling for insulation (started March '92).
