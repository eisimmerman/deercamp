Elk Mound WI

1992 - p.8.

Shot Gun Country

Nov. 24 - Day 4

We drive our vehicles down the road a ways and park.  After a round of introductions, Phil plans the first drive of the day.  The area is a mixture of fields and small woodlots.  Gene is a driver on the first drive and I stand.  The four drivers, two each from opposite ends drive at each other to create a pinch in the middle and hopefully squeeze the deer out into the field where the standers are.  No deer are pushed out but the drivers say a doe snuck out the backside of the drive and crossed the creek.

The second drive is a short one directly behind me.  I also stand on this one and Gene does as well - just a short distance away from me on a point watching the edge of the field.  I hear the drivers shouting as the drive starts and they continue to holler as they kick up deer in the swamp grass.  A stander off to my right gets three shots off so I took my safety off the 12-gauge shotgun I borrowed from Phil.  A red fox runs out of the woods, sees me, and then runs the opposite direction.  I can hear deer running so I look at the fence corner where the trail comes out.  A deer appears and I can instantly see horns!  I point the shotgun at the buck as it looks at me, turns its head to look at Gene, takes a few steps and jumps over the barb-wired fence.  As soon as its front hooves hit the ground I put the bead on the front shoulder and fired.  At the sound of the shot, the buck drops in its tracks.  It tried to get up so I fired a second shot.  Gene also shoots but misses.  I run up to the buck as it struggles on the ground and give it the final "coupe de grace" shot.  The buck turns out to be a nice 5 pointer that dresses out at 150 pounds.

For the rest of the day we made drives that push out deer but they always seem to escape from the standers.  We decide to push an island that is in the Chippewa river near the shore.  Phil got his rowboat from home and he and I take it across the river to the island.  We make the drive and push one deer off the island.  The standers let it come to the mainland and then take it.
