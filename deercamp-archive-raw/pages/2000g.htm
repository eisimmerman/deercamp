Year 7 - Larry Meyer's Land

November 19, 2000 – Day 2

The afternoon hunt of the second day is slow as well.  No deer movement over by Lorin and I.  As we get back to our meeting spot, we see that Dan is late getting back.  Duane leaves to go pick him up…another good sign?  Gene and Ben arrive, with another sad tale of Gene shooting at a doe in a snowstorm and hitting it in the foot.  Ben and Gene tracked the deer for a long time (better than 1 hour) but lost the trail in the snow. 

Duane arrives back with Dan and we all learn that Dan has shot a 6-point buck at about 4:15 p.m.   Dan has been hunting on a stand that Duane found while scouting a few years back.  It is located in an ideal deer “ambush” spot, where a thick swamp on one side is framed by thick slashing on the other side creating a funnel that brings deer right past the stand.  Since the funnel is covered with enough trees that create enough cover for bucks to feel safe when traveling through the area, deer movement is very regular all day long.  This stand produces a buck nearly every hunting season.

So…two days hunting and three bucks, not bad!  A few beers for the adults are downed and we break for night.

November 20 – Day 3

Well, back at it again for day 3.  Somehow, the eagerness to jump out of bed starts to slide away and we all move a litter slower today.  We set the alarm for 5 a.m. to get on stand and be ready to hunt by 7a.m.   Even colder today than on Sunday.  Usually that means that hunters will only sit on stand for a short time and then start deer drives around 9 a.m.   But for some reason no deer drive activity picks up today until Lorin and I start to walk out after the morning hunt around 10 a.m.
