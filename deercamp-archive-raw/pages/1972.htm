Neilsville Wisconsin

1972 - p.1.

 The "5-point buck"

Dad shot a 5-point buck on opening weekend with his Remington semi-auto model 742 30-06.  That rifle was bought back in 1968, the year dad went hunting out to Montana.  Out there he shot mule deer and antelope.  Though the gun has had to be repaired several times, it continues to be a valuable hunting partner and in the capable hands of Gene Simmerman, is still killing deer today!

Dad said that he was looking one direction and when he turned around to look the other way, he saw a deer walking with its head down.  As it raised its head he saw horns and put the crosshairs of his riflescope on the “backend” of the buck to try to run the shot lengthwise up to the chest and heart.  Apparently he “shot high” and the bullet entered the back of the deer’s head and exited out under the left eye, “dropping the deer “in its tracks”.  We had to drag the buck back out to the cemetery by going out either over or just below a beaver dam.

I vividly remember the walk in to the deer stands.  My uncle Buzz would lead the line of hunters from the campers parked at the cemetery.  We would all have our flashlights on—me, Gene, Hal, Dad, Uncle John, Tammy & Wendy, Art & Alice, Gene Melville and others? We would walk down to the beaver dam and then carefully cross it or go down below it, depending on the water level.
