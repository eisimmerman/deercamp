Abbotsford, WI

2002 - p7.

Gene and Ben Simmerman

Opening Day - Abbotsford, WI

Gene Simmerman had an opening day that few hunters ever get to experience.  Gene got up at 5:20 a.m. and then got Ben up at 5:30 a.m. and hit the road at 6:05 a.m.  They arrived at Wren Road at 6:30 a.m. As they crossed onto Wren off of Romadka, a buck crossed in front of them (rack size unknown) followed by a fork-horn buck.  Both were headed towards the beaver dam area where Gene had his stand set up.  Gene dropped Ben off at his stand and promised to pick him up at 12:30 p.m. for lunch.  

Gene then went back south a 1/4 mile to the logging road and walked in.  He got to the turn-around and parked, then walked in 160 yards through rough terrain and got to his stand about 6:45 a.m., up in it at 6:50 a.m. and ready to hunt at 7:00 a.m.  At 7:05 a.m. he heard a shot behind him, towards Ben, but closer and it made him jump.  There were probably 8 - 10 shots in the area.  Now it's 7:14 a.m. and Gene hears a noise to his right (West) and as he turned to look he saw two bucks fighting with their horns on an island across from his stand.  He then took off his right glove, put it away in his jacket, got up and rested his rifle against the tree.  Gene aimed between the trees and waited until the fork-horn buck released from the other buck.  It walked towards Gene giving him a front-shoulder shot which he took.  At the sound of the shot the buck took off running north.  As he shot through some saplings, he wasn't quite sure how well he hit it so he shot again as it was running away.  It disappeared from sight so Gene swung hi rifle back to the other buck to find it was still standing there.  Gene put the crosshairs of the scope on the buck's shoulder and shot.  It too took off running north but circled towards the back.  Again, he shot a second time at it.
