November 23 - Opening Day

running across a field by the Chippewa River, so we sped up to get to where we wanted to make the drive.  I was to be a stander again with Phil, Steve and one of his sons to be the drivers.  Well before the drive even started, Phil and Steve kicked out the three deer that had run into the strip of woods along the river, plus they pushed out a six-point buck.  Phil got off three shots at the deer but did not hit it.  So, the opening day ended with the score deer 3, hunters 0.  The drive back to Minneapolis was long and tiring, but I went to sleep early so that I could get up at three a.m. the next morning.

November 24 - Day 2

I woke up and was ready to go.  Very excited as I knew this would be my last hunting day of the 2002 season.  The temps hovering in the high 20's with clear skies and light to no winds.  It was a beautiful morning, sitting there in the darkness watching the clouds pass over the moon and waiting for the sun to rise.  The leaves on the ground were just "crunchy" enough to let a hunter know that something was coming their way.  One of the two pictures I took shows a view looking through the box blind that I was sitting in on the ground.  This was a cool experience, as I had not hunted from a stand on the ground since 1989.  

I think the first shot came around 7 a.m.  My box blind was situated in a strip of woods that was bordered on one side by a creek and three sides by fields.  It was a natural "funnel" that deer use when moving or pushed from one part of the woods to the next. On opening day I had used a deer call called "Doe Estrus Bleat".  The call was a "can" that you inverted and flipped over which then let out a sound that was to imitate a doe in heat calling out for a buck.  Well, it sure worked well in the hunting videos!  After freshening up the scent wicks, I sat down for the hunt.
