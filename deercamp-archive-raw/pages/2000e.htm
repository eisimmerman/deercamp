Year 7 - Larry Meyer's Land

November 18 – WI Deer Gun Opener

quickly got out to the road where the truck was parked.  We loaded everything in the truck and drove over to where the rest of the hunting gang would be meeting.  Tradition goes that we all meet at one spot after dark to see what success we have had and talk about the day’s hunt. 

Lorin and I were the last ones there, so there was much anticipation when we arrived…the thinking is that if you get there late, it usually is a good sign that you had taken a deer.  We found out that Travis, Dave’s son had taken a nice fork horn buck earlier in the day.  Travis had a doe come out in front of him so he let it walk to see if a buck was trailing it.  Since the rut was still underway, there was a good chance that the strategy would pay off and it did.  Others in the gang, Duane, Dan, Joe and Dave had only seen a few does and were waiting to shoot a buck.  Gene (my brother) and his son Ben had been hunting a few miles away on a 40-acre lot that Gene had shot an eight-point buck on in 1999.  I wondered if he would have success again this year and if Ben would get lucky on his first gun deer season opener.

Ben did not see any deer to shoot at but Gene had shot at a doe, wounded it in the lower gut or leg and ended up trailing it for a good distance before the blood trail vanished.  Duane offered to go help Gene look again the next day.  So, there was fun story telling all around and a few beers shared.  With a cold wind behind us and the need to register our deer before dinner, we broke up the party and agreed to get together again at lunchtime on Sunday.

November 19, 2000 – Day 2

Well, alarm goes off at 4:30 am so that we can get dressed, eat breakfast and drive to our walk-in area by 6 a.m.   Then the goal is to get on stand by 6:30 a.m. and be ready to hunt when first light breaks. 

Very cold this morning, temp has dropped down to lower 20’s with a little more wind than Saturday.  Our trail is well worn with boot prints by this time so we don’t really miss the bright eyes tacks.  All is very quiet this hunt with neither Lorin or I seeing any deer by the time we agree to get down at 10:30 a.m.   We walk out to Mr. Truck and drive back to our meeting place for lunch.  Hunters slowly roll in and the full party is ready for lunch around noon .
