Zone T Hunting Season

October 27 - Day 1

Well, since I had not shot a deer since 1997 I decided to do the T-zone hunt the last weekend of October.  On Friday afternoon Gene and Ben drove up to Pulaski Lake to stay at the Melville's trailer home.  Then they drove out to Moonridge Trail where Duane helped them set up their stands plus one for me.  Gene and Ben were able to hunt that afternoon until dark.  They did not see any deer and headed out to meet me at Paradise Shores resort near Holcombe.  I arrived at about 9:30 p.m. and after a beer we drive back to the trailer.  We get our beds and gear ready for the morning hunt and turn in.

Somehow the alarm did not go off on time so we woke up late and by the time we got out to the woods it was close to 7 a.m.  On the way in we passed two hunters who had walked in off the road and just stood or sat on the side of the logging trail.  Our walk was about half a mile back in.  The morning was just beautiful with a clear day and temps that were comfortable.  There was a little wind throughout the day as well.  

At 11:00 a.m. I decided to take a test shot at a tree to test the new scope on the gun since it had only been bore-sighted by the gunsmith and I had not taken it out to a gun range to sight it in.  Much to my surprise, I found that I had left the inline trigger lock on, so as hard as I pulled I could not shoot!  Good thing I did not see any does to shoot at.  I got down and walked over to pick up Gene, then we walked over to pick up Ben.  Upon reaching Ben, he had an interesting story to tell.  No, he did not see any does, but he did have a big 8-point buck stand broadside out in front of him for 30 minutes and
