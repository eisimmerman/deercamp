Drummond Wisconsin

1991 - p.1.

Year 2 Bayfield County

Saturday October 26 Scouting

Gene and Eric arrive at the shack at about noon.  Allen M. arrives soon after that.  Eric reviews topo maps with Gene and identifies four areas to scout.   Looks promising behind the shack near the end of the logging trail.  Across the ravine from the shack, south of the road from the shack and down a logging trail, kitty-corner from the turn to Johnnies.  We cover all the areas except the last.  Deer sign looks best behind the shack.  The end of the logging trail behind the shack divides into three points that are mostly covered by pine trees.  I pick a spot facing southwest where three trails merge.  I drop some salt and a scent dripper under a pine tree about 50 yards out from my stand.  Following the major trail Gene and I follow it until it intersects with two more trails and pick a spot on a hill looking northeast.  The stand is just off the clearing.  

Allen takes off in search of a new area somewhere beyond Club Lake.  He also plans on bow hunting until dark.  He returns after dark with a coyote as his kill.  He said he nailed it on a dead run at 30 yards.  Great shot!  Also swears he saw “bull winkle”.  Gene built and brought up a pair of single bunk beds to set in the corner of the shack.  It now sleeps six comfortably and up to eight if need be.

Sunday 10/27 Day 2 Scouting

Gene and Eric scout down the road, south down the logging road from Johnnie’s corner.  It starts to rain so we pack it in and leave about 2 p.m. 

November 9, 1 Week Before Opener

One week before the opener.  I leave Minneapolis at 6:30 a.m. and arrive at 10:30 a.m.  Appears to be at least two feet of snow on the ground.  I park at Johnnie’s corner and snowshoe to the shack, pulling my gear on a flat, roll-up toboggan.  Everything keeps falling off and I have to pick it up at least every ten yards.  I make at least five trips from the car to the shack.  Distance one way is about .6 mile.  Then, snow shoeing behind the shack takes time and I arrive at Gene’s stand around 1p.m.  Two and one-half hours just to get to the clearing behind the shack.  Setting up Gene’s ladder stand goes pretty quickly and I move on to my stand.  Hanging the tree stand goes pretty well too.  Deer have really been hitting the salt block under the pine tree.  Dirt is dug up in a circle four feet in diameter.  I find deer droppings under my tree that are 1.25” long.  According to Dr. Ken Nordberg, these would indicate a 4.5 year old buck, probably 8-12 points, a real trophy!  I manage to snowshoe out of the woods and back down porcupine to the car at Johnnies corner.  I feel ready to pass out.  So dehydrated I stop at the Triangle bar and down three tall glasses of ice water and two diet seven ups.  I leave at 4: 30 pm and get back to the cities at 8:30 p.m.
