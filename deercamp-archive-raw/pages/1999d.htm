Abbotsford, WI

1999 - p.5

Year 6 - New Land

November 20 - The Opener 

After I get to the RAV4, I climb in and start it up.  I look down the logging road to Gene and notice that it is quite wet and muddy, however, not to worry, I now am the proud owner of a SUV, and after all, this is what they are made for right?  So I point the truck down the road and step on the gas and only go about 5 feet until the nose of the truck goes straight down into a pothole and stops.  I try to back up but can’t either since the front end is pointing down so far and the back end is stuck up in the air!  JUST GREAT, not only have I missed two deer, got hit in my shooting eye with a stick, but have now gotten my truck stuck out in the woods, far from any possible help we might get.  What a day!

Fortunately Gene used my cell phone to call up Darlene's brother Larry in Colby.  Larry was about 30 minutes away and agreed to drive over to use his 4x4 truck to pull us out of our jam.  Larry arrived and hooked up the chain to the RAV4 and pulled it out of the pothole.  I spent part of the afternoon sitting in the RAV4 with my eye watering/stinging whenever I tried to open it up.  I did manage to get out on Gene's first stand and hunt for an hour or so.  At dark Gene came over from his "in-deep" stand and we walked out to the truck.  Gene drove us back to the gathering place across from the school forest and had everyone thinking he had shot a big doe.  What a surprise when Gene showed off his 8-point buck!  High fives all around.!  After a few beers to celebrate, we drove back to Colby and to Larry's barn to hang up the buck.
