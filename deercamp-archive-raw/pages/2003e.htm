Elk Mound, WI

2003 - p7.

Wisconsin Deer Season

November 22 - The WI Opener

At about 11:30 a.m. I walk across the field over to where the vehicles are parked and meet the gang for lunch.  I find out that Steve had two deer running around in the field right out in front of him and one or both of the deer may have left and come  back.  When the light was just good enough to shoot, Steve shot a buck an doe.    Neither Phil nor Dennis nor Jamie saw any deer to shoot at.  Lorin only saw the four does that came to me as they ran by him.

Phil's wife Nancy is kind enough to drive out to meet us and bring with her a lunch of hot beefs.  After lunch Phil makes a drive from across the creek with standers being Dennis, myself, Steve, Jamie and Lorin.  Phil manages to push two deer out of the drive, one was a 7 point buck that runs by Dennis and he knocks it down after a few shots.  The other deer comes out to me but stops behind some trees about 40 yards out.  While Dennis is popping off shots at his deer, this one is watching/looking that direction.  I'm not sure yet what this one is, but I know that we have a few antler less tags left to fill so I decide if I get an opening, I'll  take the shot.  After about a minute the deer is still mostly hidden and yet watching/listening in the direction of the shots from Dennis.  I think the deer is going to run soon, so I lean out to the left of where I am standing and when I see the crosshairs of the scope on the deer's chest, I squeeze the trigger and fire.  At the sound of the shot, I see the deer "jump" straight up about 3 three off the ground and then run full out to my left.  I manager to get two more shots off at it as I see it run out of view.

After Phil finishes the drive he comes over to see what I've shot at.  I tell him I think I hit the deer because it jumped straight up in the air before it ran off.  Of course Phil remembers that I am red/green color blind so he goes to look by the trees I recall the deer standing by when I shot.  He searches around for a few minutes and finally finds a piece of hair that has been "punched" out of the deer...sort of looks like the hair on a "jig" but instead of a round-shaped lead head, the end is a piece of flesh.  Phil picks up the trail of turned over leaves and tracks and follows it for a couple of hundred yards through the dry lake bed and still finds no blood trail.
