Drummond Wisconsin

1991 -  p.4.

Year 2 Bayfield County

Saturday 11/16 - The Opener

Gene joined us shortly and we all helped to dress out the deer.  We took turns dragging the deer back to camp which was quite a haul given the foot or more of snow on the ground.  Fred and Allen came back to camp late about 8 pm.  They had gone to a bar after the day’s hunt for a few beers and then gone back out to find a doe that Allen had shot but left in the woods to pick up later.  Even with the snow falling there was still a little hole in the snow where the heat from the doe’s body was escaping so even though the deer was covered up they were able to find it again.  Fred said he finally gave in to temptation after a doe fed out in front of him for 45 minutes so he shot it.  End of day 1, tally is 4 does.   After supper we went to the Triangle and Sportsman’s bars for a few drinks and then called it early and was back in the bunks by 9:30 p.m. 

Sunday 11/17 - Day 2

Temp is around 30 degrees and the wind is less than Saturday.  Fred and Allen were up and out early again.  Gene, Paul and I went off to our same stands.  The morning was quiet so Gene and I broke for lunch around 11a.m.  When we got back to the shack, we found Paul was in bed catching a nap.  He had gotten tired in the morning and came in for an early nap.  We made lunch and then turned on the Packer’s football game.  We got back out on our deer stands about 2pm and had an uneventful hunt the rest of the day.  Darkness comes at about 4:30 pm.  No deer seen by any of us around the deer shack.  

When we all came back in Allen was already back.  I put my gun away in the woodshed and came back out.  What I did not notice was a buck in one corner of the wood shed.  Apparently Allen had gotten down out of his tree stand and went to scout in a clump of thick pine trees.
