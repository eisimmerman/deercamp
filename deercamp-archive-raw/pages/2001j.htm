Abbotsford, WI

2001 - p.11.

November 17 - Opening Day

get back to the school forest and show off my buck and see what luck everyone else has had.  At 4:05 p.m. a doe again came by from the South and ran in between Lorin and I.  As all hunters know, the best times of the day to hunt are the morning and late afternoon hours, so I was on alert for more deer.  I had told Lorin the season ended at 4:45 p.m. today, so when it got to be 4:50 p.m. and he had not yet walked over, I got antsy and went to his stand.

Lorin was still up in his stand when I got there and grudgingly got down at 5:00 p.m. (Lorin correctly informed me later that actual quitting time was 5:05 p.m.).  We walked back to my tree stand and while Lorin carried the rifles and packs, I drug the buck out.  Without snow the drag was slow and hard.  We finally got out to Mr. Truck and had a beer or two.  We then drove back to the school forest to find out that not only had no one else shot any bucks, but that overall the deer movement was very slow all over. 

Everyone seemed happy on my buck kill as we spent the next 30 - 45 minutes retelling the story and just "shooting the bull".  Finally the gang broke up for the night and Lorin and I stopped in at the registration station near the hotel in Abbotsford.  

November 18 - Day Two

We got up a half-hour later today at 5:00 a.m.  We were out on the stand and ready to hunt by 6:30 a.m.  The weather forecast was for cloudy skies with a chance of rain.  High temps were to reach into the 50's.  As typical, Sunday morning hunts are much quieter with shots going off only every 5 - 15 minutes.  The morning goes along without any deer movement.  At about 8:00 a.m., I hear a really loud "boom" in the area, followed by a silence-shattering "bang" that sounds real close.  Thinking for sure that Lorin had touched off a shot from his Winchester .338, I got down and walked over to his stand.  To my surprise, Lorin was still up in his stand.  He comes down and we talk about the "boom".  Apparently, this was a sonic boom/backfire from a low-flying jet that was on a training mission.
