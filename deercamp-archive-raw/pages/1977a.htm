Neilsville Wisconsin

1977 -  p.2.

Eric’s First Buck and Ross’ Last

Dad left soon after gutting the buck to go back to his own stand.  He said that since we both shot bucks that we should start dragging them out together around noon.  Since Dad’s stand was about a mile back in the woods, it was a good idea.  Well, once Dad reached my stand with his buck, we took turns dragging one buck for a distance then switching to the other buck.  I remember it took us about two hours to get the bucks out to the cemetery.  There was no snow on the ground so the drag was very hard.  I remember feeling very relieved when I saw the yellow swamp grass that encircled the beaver dam.  That meant that we were close to getting out of the woods and not far from the cemetery.  My clothes were completely drenched with sweat and I was exhausted....even as a high-school kid in athletic shape.  Little did I know this would be my last hunt with Dad. 

As was typical of hunting down by Neilsville, we packed everything in after finishing the Sunday hunt and lunch at the Wildcat Bar.  Because of being in high school sports at the time, I needed to go back to school on Monday.  I learned later in the week my Dad was taken to the hospital complaining of chest pain.  Mom and I went down to see him but it was very hard for us because all of us were scared about what had happened.  As we left to go home, I remember my Dad saying that he loved me, but I was too afraid to look into his eyes because I had never seen him afraid of anything before.  Those were the last words I heard from my Dad as during the night he passed away.
