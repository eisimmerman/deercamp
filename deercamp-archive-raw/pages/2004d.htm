Wisconsin Deer Season

November 20 - The Opener

Well, the walk across the field seems very long in the dark.  The temp must be in the upper 30's/low 40's.  After about 10 minutes, we get to the far side of the field and find the bright eyes tacks on the trees at the edge which marks our entry point to the woods.  Now the walk seems to go faster and in no time at all Christopher and I are walking past Jamie's stand, then out into the meadow and then finally on the trail that leads to my stand.

Once at the stand, I drop my belt pack and rifle and take the doe-in-estrus lure back to the scent pads that were already in place from the scouting trip.  After soaking them we get back to my tree stand and settle in to wait for first shooting light.  Christopher decides to sit on a log at the base of the tree since the new stand I bought is about 1.5 persons wide and he was just not comfortable sitting 15 feet up off the ground.  Now the biggest challenge will be for Christopher to sit still and be quiet.  Chris does a pretty good job of this but does have frequent throat clearing, nose sniffing and movement of his legs that makes a rustling sound.  

Given that this is Christopher's first hunting experience, I have  set my goals low for this season...have a safe hunt and have fun.  Christopher sat on the ground below my tree stand and was asleep when I looked around just after first light and saw this doe very close.  She was trying to figure out what this small blaze orange blob was with blue snow pants on!  After doing the head bob, up and down and left to right a few times she decided to leave and as she turned I swung the "ought 6" around and dropped her with one shot to the neck.  She kicked around a lot so I plugged her one more time.  I then looked down to see Christopher sprawled out on the ground, face down!  Since I couldn't tell him that a deer was close and to not move or make noise I'm sure I surprised him with the first shot!  He asked me what I did and I told him I shot a deer.
