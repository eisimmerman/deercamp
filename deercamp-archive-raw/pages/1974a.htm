Neilsville Wisconsin

1974 -  p.2.

  Gene Simmer man’s First Buck

Dragging the buck out down the railroad tracks was hard and eventually wore away some hair off the hide on the hindquarters.  After getting the buck out, we drove down to the Wildcat Inn bar to watch the Packer's football game and then went back to Snyder county park to pack up and pull our trailer home.

Also, Big Gene tells me that one time a doe was approaching and Little Gene kept letting it come closer and closer.  When it seemed that the doe would either discover Little Gene or run, Big Gene shot, followed immediately by a shot from Little Gene.  Apparently, it sounded like this, BOOM BANG.

I’m not sure of the year, but I wanted to capture how deer hunting went after we spent the opening weekend in Neilsville.  We spent the rest of the week hunting around Cornell with the hunting gang that was made up mostly of Simmerman relatives.  Others included were Buzz’s son Randy, Tammy’s boyfriend, Eddie Thon and others I can’t recall.  I do recall that Gene and I spent a lot of time bouncing around in the back of our 1974 Dodge Power Wagon truck as we drove from one drive to another.
