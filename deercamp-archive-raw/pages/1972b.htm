Neilsville Wisconsin

1972 -  p.3.

 The "5-point buck"

 

Then a hundred yards further was the stand for Gene and I.  We would sit somewhere near a trail hub by thick cover to the North and well-worn trails to South.

 If you kept going another quarter mile or so, you would come to Dad’s stand that was atop a knoll that was seated between a swamp and a draw.  Just before Dad's stand was another stand that our brother Hal sat on for a couple of years.

Gene Melville was the last person in to his stand, which was almost out to the other side of the square, just inside the railroad tracks.  In all, I’m sure it was at least one mile to walk in.
