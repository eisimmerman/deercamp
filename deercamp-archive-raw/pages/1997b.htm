Abbotsford, WI

1997 - p.3

Year 4

Saturday, November 23  – Day 2

The weather turned cold overnight and the temperature was in the high 20's.  Wind was out of the north to northwest so it was blowing right in my face.  At 9:35 a.m. I hear a sound of a deer running in my direction.  I look north at a big pine tree that has a deer trail by it and in a few seconds a deer comes out on a full run headed right at me.  I immediately see that the deer is a buck with a long spike on the left side and no horn on the right.  I pick out an opening by a tree and hold my scope on the opening and shoot when I see the buck come into view.  It drops in its tracks!  My first legal spike buck was taken at 9:40 a.m. at 40 yards with my Remmington 7MM magnum bolt action rifle.  

Today is also a special day to shoot a buck on.  It is the 20-year anniversary of my father's death, which happened during deer season in 1977.  I get down at 10:30 a.m. and drag the buck out to the road and then back down to the old base camp in the school forest since that is where Gene parked his car and would meet me for lunch.  After congratulations, we load the buck into the trunk of the car and drive over to the new meeting place to join the others for lunch.  Duane is excited for me since this is my first buck since Gene and I began hunting with him in 1994.  

We break out the gas grill and cook some venison on the tailgate of Duane's truck while listening to the Packers football game on the radio.  We finish and get back out to hunt in the early afternoon.

November 25  – Nov 27 - Days 3 - 5

I don't recall much action happening or anyone taking a deer over the next three days, so will close this season and look forward to 1998.
