Abbotsford, WI

1998 - p.2

Year 5

We have more than a few cold beers to lament our bad luck and to cool off from the heat.  Well, there's always another hunt tomorrow.

Monday - Tuesday - Nov 23 & 24 - Days 3 & 4  

Well, it is getting very tiring looking at the same old woods without seeing any deer.  Nothing new to report here.

Wednesday - Nov 25 - Day 5

After the morning hunt with no results, we try a deer drive in the school forest through the thick dog-hair like saplings that have grown up after the clear-cut in 1996.  Duane, Gene and myself all go post as standers as Dan makes a one-person drive by weaving back and forth (north and south) through the brush as the deer could hide in this stuff only five feet away and you would never see it as the brush is so thick.  Dan gets almost all the way out of the drive and nearly stumbles on a doe.  It runs between the standers and no one sees it.  So, the 1998 season ends for me without seeing a single deer and without taking a single shot.  Though I feel sad about ending the hunt I still enjoyed my time out in the woods.  

As I have always done, I hunt for memories, not for trophy racks or meat.  This season certainly was memorable.  I head back to the twin cities just before lunch.
