Drummond Wisconsin

1991 -  p.3.

Year 2 Bayfield County

Saturday 11/16 - The Opener

Alarm goes off at 4:30 a.m.  and I jump out of the rack.  As is usual, the wood fire in the barrel stove has died out during the night and the room temp is cold.  Stepping out onto the icy linoleum my feet feel like ice blocks.  I fire up the stove while Fred gets some coffee going.  Fred leaves soon for Clam Lake and Allen shortly after.  Gene and Paul roll out of the bunk beds and slowly get around.  We walk down the logging road and Gene and Paul split off for their stands.  I continue down a few hundred yards further and climb up into my stand.  As daylight breaks I can see that the deer have just hammered the salt block that is about 50 yards away.  The morning is slow, with little shooting and deer movement.  I don’t recall but the three of us may have gone in for lunch and came back out to hunt in the early afternoon.. 

Late in the afternoon the snow picked up and was falling pretty heavily.  During that time a big doe and a fawn ran by my stand and quickly up the hill that I couldn’t get a shot off.  With the snow on the ground and falling, it was almost impossible to hear anything coming.  Well, I was sure that my opportunity for a shot had been blown, so I sat back and relaxed in the tree stand.  A few minutes later a hunter unloaded 5 shots just yards behind me.  Apparently Paul had been sitting on his stand watching one direction, when he looked back he saw a couple of deer feeding away from him.  He decided to get up and see if he could stalk within shooting distance.  When Paul finally got close enough, he shot all five shots at the two deer, finally dropping the last one as it ran directly at him.  This was all less than 40 yards behind me so I was very surprised when it turned out to be Paul.
