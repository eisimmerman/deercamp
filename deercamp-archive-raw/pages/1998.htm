Abbotsford, WI

1998

Year 5

November 14  – Pre-season Scouting

We end up putting my tree stand up in the same spot as in 1997.  The woods are quite a bit more grown up in just a year.  It will be harder to see the deer unless there is some snow on the ground.  So far, November has been quite warm, with temps above average.  We put Gene's stand back in the school forest and although the sign looks good, the saplings and brush have become really thick, so seeing deer back in there will be hard too.   

Saturday, November 21  – The Opener

Very quiet this morning, not much shooting going on anywhere.  Temps are warm and what little snow cover we had on the ground has melted.  Just after 7 a.m. a hunter walks in off Cardinal and sits down about 100 yards away from me.  I try to get his attention to let him know that he is too close, but either he doesn't care or does not see me.  The rest of the day goes about the same and at the end of the day after dark, we all meet to talk about the day and discuss Sunday's hunt.  Very few deer are seen, only a few does goes by Dave's son Travis.  After a few beers we break for the night.  

 Sunday, November 22  – Day 2

More of the same...temps too warm, too windy to hear anything moving and that makes for deer hunters that don't move around and deer that stick to heavy cover when the wind is high.  Not a deer seen by 
Gene or myself and few deer seen by anyone else.  At least we have the Packers football game and some venison to grill at lunch to break up the slow hunting.  The afternoon hunt is just as bad.  We all can't believe it, by now someone should have had a shot at a buck or doe, but the deer just aren't moving.
