Eau Claire, WI

2000 - p.12.

Shot Gun & Rifle Country

December 9 - Day 1 Extended Season

We all talk after that drive and then we move to do another drive where we think the doe ran.  Nothing gets pushed out, however, when making the drive, Arnie and Dennis find a deer bed that is soaked with blood from a wounded deer.  They send me back to another stand and tell me to be sure to watch behind myself as if the deer gets pushed it most likely will run behind me.  The drive is in progress when I hear Arnie and Dennis talking so I turn around to watch behind me.  Suddenly, I hear a stick snap and then see out of the corner of my vision the brown/gray shape of a deer running from my right to left.  I put the scope of my rifle on an opening and wait for the deer to enter my field of view and then squeeze off a shot.  The deer veers off and keeps running.  I hear Arnie ask who shot and hear Dennis’answer that it must have been Eric, based on where the shot came from.

The drivers arrive and we look at where the deer was when I shot and find some blood so there must have been a hit.  Dennis and I track the deer to find it has crossed the creek and up into the other side.  Arnie gets a call on his cell phone telling us that Gene and Ben have arrived at the farm, so his suggestion is that we let the deer lie down and then go after it when we have more hunters.  However, Dennis keeps tracking the deer and gets off another shot at it.  I join him and we track it for 30 more minutes as it keeps circling around.  We never did see the deer again or push it out on another drive.

We all load up and drive over to shotgun country, have lunch and then plan the afternoon’s drives.  We make the same drive that I shot my buck on back in 1992.  The drive pushes out a doe or two by Arnie’s sons who get off a few shots but do not connect.  We make one more drive along the river with no success.  Finally, we all gather back by the vehicles and wrap up the day by just shooting the B.S.  As daylight falls, we end the season (for myself, Gene and Ben) and drive back home.
