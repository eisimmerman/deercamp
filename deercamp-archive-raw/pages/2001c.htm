Abbotsford, WI

2001 - p.4.

Pre-season Scouting

November 10 - School Forest Area

after the usual greetings Duane states, "well, besides setting up our stands today, the first thing we are going to do is go find us a buck"  Not being clear on what Duane has in mind I ask and he replies that Dan had hit a buck bow hunting Friday night, tracked it for a ways and kicked it up, only to have it run off again.  So Dan had backed off and let the buck lay up overnight.  The thinking was that the buck would bleed out and die, making it easier to find the next day.  So, Gene, Ben and I jump into his truck and follow Duane and Dan in their truck over to the school forest.  We walk down a logging road and then Duane has us spread out to walk from West to East.  We walk only about 10 yards apart as the brush is as thick as "the hair on a dog".  Duane correctly recalls that I am red/green color blind, so he puts me on the edge of the swamp and hardwoods with the instructions to "just look for a dead deer body"!  Gene, Ben, Dan, Duane and I then begin our slow crawl through the brush until Gene finds Dan's broken arrow on the trail.  

The temperature is warm, probably in the high 30's/low 40's by now, so the blood is not frozen and appears fresh.  It could also be that we kicked up the buck when we first entered the brush.   At times everyone gets down on their hands and knees to pick up the trail when they lose it.  Separately Gene, Ben, Dan and Duane all take their turn picking up the trail when it appears that it has veered off or stopped.  Finally, Dan goes on ahead, stops, then yells out , "here it is!  The buck had made one last attempt to jump over a dead stump but instead collapsed on top of it.  A big-bodied six pointer, high fives all around!  There was an unusual black streak of black hair down the buck's neck, almost like a mane.  In total, the buck must have run about a quarter of a mile from where Dan had hit it - a 17 yard shot.  When we returned to his stand Dan dug out the broad head form the dirt.
