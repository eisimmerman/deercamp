Elk Mound, WI

2002 - p4.

Day Trip Hunting

November 24 - Day 2

With two brow tines that were 1 inch long it ended up being a 5-point buck.  So, I dropped my gear and got my knife out and a pair of rubber gloves figuring that would protect me if the deer had CWD.  All things considered, it appeared to be a healthy deer so I was not worried.  After gutting out the deer in 20 minutes it was time to take some pictures.  After that I propped open the gut cavity of the buck to let it cool down.  Needless to say, the rest of the morning hunt I was not at all cold as I'm sure I had adrenaline pumping through my veins.   

At about 8:00 a.m. Steve walked over to see what I had shot.  As he crossed the field from his corner stand and came into the woods, I pointed to where my buck was down.  He walked over to it and stood there until I came over.  He was surprised that it was a buck as this deer, along with a bigger deer, had walked across the open dirt field in front of him, allowing him to "scope" the two deer for about 5 minutes.  Because the background of the field was dark dirt and with the sun in his eyes from an east-facing stand, he did not see the horns on the buck that I shot.  The bigger deer had left my deer and crossed over in front of him to the left and entered the cedars.  The deer that I shot had continued to his right and entered the woods by me.  I don't know if the timing was just that the buck came by me minutes after I used the bleat call or if using the call "lured" him in to my woods while the other deer went the other direction.

Around 9:30 a.m. I heard some shooting that was close so I thought someone else in the hunting party must have shot.  The rest of the morning was quiet with me just enjoying being out in the woods.  Around 10:30 a.m. Phil and Steve came over to get me and to look at my buck.  Phil said that he and his son Dennis had gotten up and made a "two-man" drive at about 9 a.m. While pushing the woods they saw two does which they promptly shot.  After dressing them out, they dragged them out closer to the field but left them
