November 8 - The MN Opener

and it takes me about one hour to drag the doe out.  I end up walking about 100 steps, resting for a minute and then walking another 100 steps.  About 3/4 of the way out another hunter catches up with me on the trail and gives me a hand - thanks!  I get the deer in the van and stop back at the farm to show it off.  Then it's on to Pine City to register it.  The process is quite different than in Wisconsin.  Here they just take your word on what you have brought in and issue you a tag for you to put it on the deer yourself.

I end up skinning and butchering the doe myself at home...took about 4 - 5 hours but was fun for the kids and myself.

November 16 - WI Pre-Season Scouting

Lorin Toepper and I meet Phil at his farm in Elk Mound, WI.  Mistakenly, I give Lorin directions to Phil's farm as if he were coming from the Twin Cities.  This "throws" Lorin off by a good 30 - 45 minutes (due to a small side-trip to Colfax!) but he eventually gets there.  After greetings all around we talk about driving over to rifle country to set up our tree stands.  I decide to move my stand from 2002 and let Lorin set up where I shot my buck in 2002.  Lorin bought a new tree stand that was quite tall and needed all three of us to put it up.  

Phil and I took off to go find a tree to put my portable stand up in while Lorin was finishing the seat/shooting rail assembly and adding more tie-down straps.  Phil and I went north towards the cedars and then east toward a logging road that separates his property from one of his neighbors.  There is a spot in there that Phil's son Chris has sat at before.  We finally find a tree and I put the screw-in steps in and then haul the tree stand up to attach it.
