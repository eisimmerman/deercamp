Drummond Wisconsin

1992 - p.6.

Year 3 Bayfield County

Nov. 21  - The Opener

Gene says he will go on tracking and that I should wait on this side of the creek so we know where the deer came from and how to get back to the logging trail.  As Gene disappears up and over the other side of the hill, it gets very dark and quiet.  After what seems like forever, I call out to check on if he sees anything.  Gene eventually comes back to the creek and says he found the doe about 100 yards in and that he was going back to drag it to the creek.  He drags it down the side and I cross the creek to help him bring it over.  Then up the bank again and a couple hundred yards out to the logging trail.  Man, we were wiped out after getting to the logging trail so we sat down and had a beer and took a few pictures.  Then back another quarter mile to the hunting shack and we’re there.  We hung the doe up on the meat pole - first deer!  Weather is warm - about 36 degrees with the snow melting.

Nov. 22  - Day 2

Four a.m. and I'm the first one out of the rack again.  With the styrofoam insulation in the ceiling, it stays warm overnight and there is no need for a fire in the morning.  Fred takes off at 4:45 a.m. and says he will leave from his stand as he has to work on Monday.  Gene and I hunt behind the shack until 10:30 a.m. and then take off to follow what we believe is the blood trail of the second doe I shot at that also ran west of the creek.  We trudge up and down the hills and through thick brush.  Gene keeps on going, saying, "just one more hill!"  The blood trail stayed even and only in the tracks of the deer.  After about 1 - 2 miles we give up and turn around.  Gene is exhausted coming back to the creek so we rest often.  Finally we get to the creek and I notice a patch of deer hair and a deer hoof on the ground.   I pull on the hoof and find it buried under a pile of debris.  Gene pulls it out and we find a spike buck that was badly decomposed.
