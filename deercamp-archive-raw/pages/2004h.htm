Wisconsin Deer Season

November 21 - Day 2

We get up a half-our later and get out to rifle by 6:00.  Today, Christopher and I will set in the box blind location where I shot a 5-point buck in 2002.  There has been no one in this stand so far and Phil notes that most of the deer that have come through have passed through this area.  Chris and I get on stand and are ready to hunt by 6:30 a.m. 
The morning is cold--in the high 20's.   After 30 minutes, Chris tells me he wants to go back to the vehicle.  Finally, I take him out and get back out to the box blind by 7 a.m.  

Five minutes later I hear seven or eight shots coming from Steve's direction.  Soon Christopher comes back out and we sit in the box blind until about 8:30 a.m. and then decide to walk over to Steve's stand.  In the past half-hour I have seen Steve walking out to the middle of the field that his stand sits on, so I think Steve must have shot something.  When Christopher and I get there, we find Phil, Dennis and Steve standing over an 11-point buck, wow!  Steve also states he has shot a 7-pointer that is just in the edge of the woods.  Man, this buck is a beauty.  

I may have indirectly pushed these two bucks to Steve as they came out between us into the field just after I came back to the box blind.  Had I been at the box blind 5 minutes earlier, well...who knows?

We next make three drives but push no deer out.  We talk about making deer drives in shotgun country and then make the usual drives...Ness', along the river, etc.  We push a doe and a buck out.  Dennis gets a shot at the buck but only has the brisket to shoot at.  The doe runs out across the field and three of the standers get shots at it but no one connects.  By 4 p.m. we are done for the day.
