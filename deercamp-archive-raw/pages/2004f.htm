Wisconsin Deer Season

November 20 - The Opener

At about 10:00 a.m. I heard some shooting behind me back towards the direction that Dennis would be sitting.  I kept looking back that way for a few minutes and after about 5 minutes I turned back to look in front of me..  At about 10:05 a.m. I heard the sound of a deer running my direction.  

As I stared intently, I picked out the shape of a deer that stopped behind some trees.  At this point, I did not know if it was a buck or doe.  A minute later I could hear Phil shouting to Dennis as they apparently were on the trail of a deer that Dennis shot at.  That was enough to push the deer I was watching towards me and as it started running again, I could see it was a buck!  

I quickly looked ahead to see where my shooting lanes would be and saw that they were very limited.  If I was lucky, the buck would stop in the exact spot where I could get a shot at its neck.  Getting any shot at the buck would be a challenge since it was likely to run by me just below the river bank level meaning ONLY neck and head would be visible.  Sure enough when the buck took off it ran this course.

Well, the buck stopped in the exact spot where I could put the crosshairs of my scope on its neck and fire . Bang! I hit it. It took off running and I swung the scope on it's chest and fired again, it dropped! It started kicking and trying to get up so I shot a third time (hmmm...seems like a habit now doesn't it?!) The third shot unfortunately hit it in the hind quarters but did put the buck down for good. I let out a loud "yeah" when it dropped. No sooner than that I heard Phil yell out, "did you get it"? I said yes and saw him and Dennis come from behind my stand. Apparently Dennis had hit the buck in the chest with his 30-30 rifle but did not put it down.  When I looked down at Chris, this time he was lying on his back on the ground!
