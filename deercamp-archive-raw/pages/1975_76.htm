Neilsville Wisconsin

1975 - 1976

A new hunter joins the gang

Nineteen seventy-six was my sophomore year in high school.  I invited my friend Lorin Toepper to go deer gun hunting with us.  Lorin had relocated to Cornell from McHenry Illinois and this was his first time whitetail hunting.  I remember Lorin calling in on the walkie-talkie at 9 a.m. and stating that he had seen some deer but he did not shoot at them because they were in the “brush”.  “Of course they are in the brush, I told Lorin, we’re out in the woods!  At the next call in Lorin said he had shot a deer and that it had run off.  My dad and I went over to his stand to help track the deer and found that Lorin had wounded a buck fawn.  We were able to track it down and after missing the first “coupe de grace” shot, I finally put it down.

I remember being very amused at the hunting clothing that Lorin first wore.  It seemed as though he thought he could just dress for a casual winter's walk on the Navy Pier in Chicago by the lake.  I recall he wore a pair of blue jeans (not sure if he had any "long johns" on) and his hiking boots that he would wear to school each day.  Well, to his credit, if he did get cold, he didn't let on.  In those days we hunted from the ground by sitting either on a small fold-up stool or a pile of sticks.

Lorin used Ross' 12-gauge semi-auto Remington model 1100 shotgun.  I know that when you shot slugs through the gun it did have quite the kick.  Gene and I both used bolt-action 30-30 Steven's rifles with open sites.
