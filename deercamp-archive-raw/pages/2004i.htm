Elk Mound, WI

2004 - p10.

Wisconsin Deer Season

November 22 - Day 3

Up at 5:30 a.m. and out at Ness' to talk about the first deer drive for the day.  We decide to make the drive behind Norman's house as Phil knows of a big buck that has been hiding out there.  We try to time two drives at one time, one starting behind Norman's house and working towards the bridge and the other drive starting in Ness's and working towards the bridge in a pincher movement.  We end up not pushing any deer out.  

We gather the hunters up and talk about making another "pincher style" drive with dual driver teams working at the same time.  One group started up on the "hill" and worked down towards highway 85.  I was a driver with the second group that was making a push through some tall grass and through a dry creek bed.  Again, the drive fails to produce any deer movement.  

We made a drive in the afternoon along the Chippewa river when Phil kicks up a doe just within yards of where I  am standing.  I hear Phil's voice clearly, something like, "deer, doe coming".  Within a few seconds I see a doe jumping and running through the tall grass along the river.  It runs by me at about 15 feet away and I am so surprised by how quickly it appears and how close it is I miss the first shot and the follow-up three shots.  I manage to take a chunk out of a tree, but that is the only thing I hit!

Next, Christopher and I were drivers working the big woods.  Chris took along a stick and was banging it on the trees as we walked.  Apparently the only thing we pushed out was about 20 turkeys to Joe.  Finally, we made two drives, one to push the woods along the river up to the island and the last one was where Joe and Dennis actually took a row boat across to the island.  Sometimes this is a great hiding spot when deer get pressured but no deer are moved off it today.
