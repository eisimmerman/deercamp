Drummond Wisconsin

1992 - p.1.

Year 3 Bayfield County

Thurs. 3/5 - Post-Season Scouting

Eric arrived up in Drummond on Thursday night so that he could get a full-day scouting on Friday before Gene came up for the weekend.  The drive down Porcupine was ok.  I spent the night reading and drinking some beers.  

Fri. 3/6 - Post-Season Scouting

First stop in the morning is Corbow, a once-active hunting camp started by someone from my hometown of Cornell, WI.  It looks run down and like no one has used it or stayed there for years.  It was located just behind the Triangle Bar.  After looking around for an hour I head off to explore another potential hunting area on the topo map.  Later that afternoon I head back to the hunting shack off Porcupine drive.

Sat. 3/7 - Post-Season Scouting

We decide to try some post-season scouting and shed hunting.   Gene and Eric head out to scout behind the hunting shack.  Our plan is to go all the way North to the point and then comb the area East to West in 10-yard grids.  We found at least 30 deer beds under the pine trees on the point.  More deer droppings of one inch are found -- looks like "Bullwinkle" is still around!

We came to Gene's stand off the clearing and decided to take a break.  As we were standing around I looked down to where the salt block had been and there on the ground was a perfect 5-point shed!  Very cool.  We look around the salt-block area closely for the other shed but come up empty.

The landscape looks quite beautiful during the winter.  We took several pictures of 18-mile creek and the unusual snow and ice formations.
