Wisconsin Deer Season

November 7 - Elk Mound Scouting

On Sunday morning I get up at 7 a.m. and try to get Christopher and Claire up and around to come with me.  Christopher decides he does not want to go and Claire is slow to get going, so I don't leave the house until about 8:30 a.m.  Though I'm tired from the short night of sleep and the long day hunting the day before, I am still very excited to be going to WI to set up my new tree stand-- a 15' ladder stand that has a wrap-around shooting rail so I can steady my shots.

At about 9:30 a.m. I call Phil and let him know that I've just passed Menomonee.  Phil tells me to go directly out to the woods in rifle country so I manage to remember where to go.  Phil worked hard on the fall harvest and finally did get all the corn down in the big field.  Both Nancy and Phil are waiting for us by the stand on the corner of the field.  I drive the Mazda Tribute slowly through the field to get there.  

First order of the day is to go down to the creek and rebuild the foot bridge that crosses over.  Each spring the creek water level gets high enough that it washes out most of the bridge, so re-construction each fall is usually needed.  Phil has brought along scrap lumber from the farm which we use to repair the bridge.  Phil decides he needs another long tree to be placed in the bridge so that it will be stable and have something more to nail the planking to.  We find a tree just the right size and length right by the bridge so with his chainsaw Phil cuts and drops the tree right in place!  After a few minutes of work, we have a nice footbridge which should serve us well this fall.
