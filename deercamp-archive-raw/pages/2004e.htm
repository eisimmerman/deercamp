Elk Mound, WI

2004 - p6.

Wisconsin Deer Season

November 20 - The Opener

I climbed down and we walked the 15 yards over to the doe and tagged it.  Since it was only 7:10 a.m., I did not want to spend a lot of time gutting the deer out as it was still prime time hunting.  So back we went to the tree stand.  I offered Chris a chance to sit up in the stand with me but it was not quite wide enough for him to be comfortable so he asked to go sit on the ground.  

Well there was not much action up to 9 a.m. and since I had not seen a buck I decided to get down and go gut out the doe.  I first described to Chris how the process would go and then stuck my knife into the guts of the doe.  Since it had been sitting there for over 90 minutes, it had started to bloat and when the knife point punctured the guts the abdomen let out a big "whoosh" followed by some nasty/stinky smells!  Chris almost fell over in the woods from the stench!  I finished gutting the doe and with the entrails out on the ground Chris seemed to have fun poking at different parts with my knife to see what was inside.  We finished up and together drug the doe out to the logging trail for later pick up.

I then gave Chris the option of sitting up in the stand while I sat on the ground.  Due to the 15 foot height, Chris did not like the idea and did not last for more than 5 minutes up there.  So we exchanged places.  By 9:45 a.m. we were both back in our places.
