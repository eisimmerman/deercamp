November 18 – WI Deer Gun Opener

A chilly morning, temp somewhere is the mid to upper 20’s.  The “bright eyes” tacks are still on the trees, so the walk in goes smoothly.  I get on my stand and am ready to hunt by 6:30 a.m.   As usual, I hear the first shot off in the distance before legal shooting light…seems like someone does this every year just to claim the honor.  I see no deer or hear any local shots until 8am .  Between 8 am and noon I see a total of 6 deer, all does I think.  Usually the deer are running by fast so I can just verify they are deer.  My stand is facing north and I expect deer to come from the south or southeast for the most part.  Around 10 am two deer come from the east, at a fast trot or slow run.  I decide to pick out the lead deer.   It is hard finding a spot to shoot at since they are running directly at me.  I wait for the lead deer to turn and place the crosshairs of the scope on the shoulder and squeeze off a shot.  The deer drops to the ground and is kicking wildly as it tries to get back up.  I expect the deer will die in a few seconds do I decide to wait.

 As I watch the deer for what seems like 30 seconds, it slowly gets back to its feet!  It starts to run away and I get off three more shots at it, but with no luck.
