Abbotsford, WI

2001 - p.7.

Pre-season Scouting

November 10 - School Forest Area

on Sunday 11/11 and put up Ben's stand.  Gene, Ben and I decide to go down the road to find the logging road to sight in our rifles.  We each take turns firing off a few shots at a target about 40 yards away.  Gene shoots first, taking 6 shots which line up from Northeast to Southwest of the bulls eye, all perfectly in a row with a span of no more than three inches around the bulls eye.  I shoot next and after adjusting the scope, I place three shots over the center of the bulls eye, about one inch high.  Ben wraps up by shooting his 20-gauage shotgun and putting  a few shots right on target.  As the light begins to fade with the sun setting around 5 pm, I get into my Mazda Tribute and point it back west towards Minneapolis.  

November 16 - Final Setup

Lorin was going to drive up Friday and meet me at the woods to put up his stand, so I drove over from Minneapolis and got to the woods at 11 a.m.  As I walked in, I noticed that many of the  bright eyes tacks I had put up were gone.   Also, the scent dripper I had put out near my stand was gone.  Fortunately, my stand had not been touched.  Lorin arrived about noon so we put his stand up about 150 yards from me.  Since our fiasco of putting up Lorin's stand from last year, I had been thinking about how to keep the stand upright as you climb up to secure it.

Well I cam up with the bright idea of tying a strap loosely around the tree so that as we lifted it up to lean it against the tree, we could secure the bottom straps and move up the ladder.  If the top of the stand shifted it would still catch and hold onto the tree.  So, the stand went up pretty fast and easy (although the takedown would later come back to challenge us).  On the way back out to the road we put up more bright eyes tacks and got out of the woods around 2 p.m.  We decided to just hang out for a while and enjoyed the sunny weather and a beer.  We then drove to the hotel and checked in.
