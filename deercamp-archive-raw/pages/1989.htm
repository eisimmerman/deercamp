Neilsville Wisconsin

1989

Our last Deer Season in Unit 55, Clark County

This season was, unknown to us then, to be our last deer season in Neilsville.  Missing another buck made it memorable as well.  I was hunting on Hal’s old stand and Gene S. was on Dad’s old stand.  I was standing and looking to the East.  Again, it was before 9 a.m. when I heard a deer running from the West (behind me) along the edge of the swamp that ran past me up past Dad’s stand.  When I turned to look, I saw the deer was a fork horn buck.  It stopped in a clearing, allowing me to shoulder my rifle against a tree for a steady shot.  I put the crosshairs on the buck’s chest area and shot.  It just looked at me and ran off!  I couldn’t believe it!  Well, once again I blame the “extra fine” crosshairs on that old Redfield scope that was on the 30-06 that made it hard to see where you were aiming.  That, and a quick shot combined with “looking at the whole deer” and not picking out a spot probably contributed to the missed shot.

This hunting season was also a memorable one for my brother Gene.  Later that day, while still hunting from Dad's stand, he saw a deer far off to the southeast.  Gene shot and apparently knocked it down, but it got up and ran off.  Later, Gene and Allen M. trailed the deer, found it and finished it off.  The deer was a doe-fawn that Gene had first hit in the lower legs at 110 yards.  Even though the deer was a fawn, we always tagged the deer we shot. 

Since the clear cutting of Unit 55 in 1984 really cut down the available land to hunt on, it forced the rest of the hunters into smaller parcels of land, which in the case of Allen, Gene M.  and Paul meant that it was more common to have other hunters around you than before.  That usually led to fewer deer sightings and a lower quality hunt.  I don’t remember exactly, but Allen may have said that this was his last hunt there because of all the hunters that were crowding him out.
