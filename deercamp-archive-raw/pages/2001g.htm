Abbotsford, WI

2001 - p.8.

Day Before The Opener/Opening Day

November 16 - Final Setup

At night we had a few (more) beers and ate pizza at the local pizza hut.  Back at the motel we prepared our meals for the next  day and got our gear ready.  We set the alarm clock for 4:30 a.m. and watched a movie until we fell asleep.

November 17 - Opening Day

Up at 4:30 a.m., actually up before that as I always don't sleep very well due to the anticipation of the next day.  I typically "wake up" on the hour and look at the clock to see what time it is.  My greatest fear is that I oversleep and miss the opening morning hunt...that would be a disaster as probably 25% of the total deer kill is taken on opening day, and probably half of that is taken opening morning.

We get dressed, get in Mr. Truck and drive out to the woods.  We arrive at our walk-in spot about 5:45 a.m. and decide to wait until 6:00 a.m. as it is still very dark.  Finally we walk in  and notice right away that the "local yokels" have pulled another stunt and arranged all the bright eyes tacks they had taken down in a "smiley face" design on one of trees.  Somehow, I failed to see the humor!  Nevertheless, we walk in to my stand without incident and then Lorin continues on to his stand.  I wait at the bottom of my tree until it gets a little lighter, however it appears that a fog has settled in which will cause it to stay darker in the woods as legal shooting time arrives at 6:30 a.m.

Once it gets light enough to see the season starts out very slowly with few shots around us.  As usual, it seems the "action" or shooting is somewhere in the distance.  I see no deer movement up to the magical time of 9:00 a.m...which in past hunts has been the milestone by which I measure my success in shooting a buck.  If I have not seen or shot at a buck by then, I usually won't see one the rest of the season.
