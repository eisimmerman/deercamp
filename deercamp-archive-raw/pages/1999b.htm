Year 6 - New Land

November 20 - The Opener 

Both deer then ran off heading northwest.  I could not believe it, two missed shots at deer standing in the open only 50 yards away.  

By now I was feeling really down.  After talking to Gene later in the morning, he said he was sure that I would have had two deer down by now, given all the shooting I had as well as the fact that in past hunting seasons, I usually have killed my deer with one shot. 

So, after all that early action I settle back into my tree stand and try to calm down.  Then, around 9 a.m., I hear a shot from over Gene’s way.  I listen closely for the follow-up “coupe de grace” but nothing comes until I hear a loud “YEAH”.  I get down from my stand and hurry over to Gene’s stand to find him on the ground standing next to an 8-point buck!  Apparently the buck was running right at him and then took a right turn onto a trail in front of him, giving Gene the chance for a running broadside shot.  He hit the buck in the back part of the lungs and dropped it with one shot!

We were both thrilled.  This was only Gene’s second buck in his 25 years of hunting.  He has always been in the wrong place at the right time so it was very cool that he got this buck.  After congratulations and pictures, Gene dressed out the buck.  Since it was after 10 a.m., we decided that we would leave the buck at his stand and go back in northeast to the far stand and do a better job of marking the stand trail walking out to the logging road.  When we first did this on scouting weekend, we mistakenly got off course and wandered onto the neighbor’s land that was posted, so we needed to redo the bright eyes tacks or tape flags anyway.
