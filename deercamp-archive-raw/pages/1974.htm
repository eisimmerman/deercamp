 Gene Simmerman's First Buck

Big Gene (Gene Melville) and little Gene (Gene Simmerman) went in all the way to the back of the square to their stands. There stands were just off the railroad tracks not far from the #9 marker.   Little Gene decided to hunt near Big Gene as there was more deer movement going on.  Early on in our hunts in Unit 55 we began using walkie talkies, before it became illegal, to talk to each other when hunting with a party tag (group of 4 hunters) that allowed us to shoot does.  I remember our first “call in” was usually at 9a.m. and then hourly after that.  Sometimes it was so cold that I could barely move my mouth to speak. 

Well it was Sunday of opening weekend and we were coming out of the woods around noon because we typically took the afternoon off to go watch the Packers football game at the Wildcat bar.  While walking down the railroad tracks to go pick up the others, we heard Big Gene say over the walkie talkie, “you better get on over here, your son just got his first buck”.  Tradition goes that when a hunter gets the first buck, a “smear” of blood from the buck in wiped across the forehead of the lucky hunter.  So, Dad did this to Gene

So, here goes the story about how Gene shot his first buck.  According to Big Gene, Little Gene was busy munching away on candy bars, “rattle, rattle, crunch, crunch when he looked up and saw the buck staring at him.  Now I don’t remember if he shot once or twice, but it has been verified that Gene made the killing shot while on the run!
