Neilsville Wisconsin

1972 -  p.2

 The "5-point buck"

Then it was up a short hill and over a series of rolling hills.  We were careful to keep up on the side or top of the ridge with the swamp or lowlands down below on our left.  The direction we walked in was East.  Somehow Ross or his brother Buzz were always able to lead us directly in to each of our stands.  Back then we did not use any sort of markers for the stand trails, no tape or tacks or blazes.

Eventually we would come to a spot on the ridge you came to that was across from a big pine tree down in the swamp.  This was John’s stand.  The picture shown here was taken sometime after the hunting land was clear cut.  This view is looking back West towards the direction of the beaver dam and cemetery.  The ridge would have been up and to the left.

Just down the ridge was Buzz’s stand, next to a birch tree that was lying horizontal, about 25 feet across.  For many years it was my landmark to finding that stand.  

Continuing East, the trail led to Gene, Eric and Ross's stands.
