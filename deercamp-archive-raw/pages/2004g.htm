Wisconsin Deer Season

November 20 - The Opener

I gutted the buck out and Chris got the honor of hanging up the buck's testicles in a tree to show other hunters a buck had been killed there.  Dennis drug it out to the logging trail.  Chris and I sat for about one hour more and then walked back to the meeting place for lunch at 11:30 a.m.Chris and I met Steve at our lunch spot.  Since it was mid-day, we both decided to put our rifles down at the lean-to (big mistake!).  As Steve and I were talking, Steve looked out and saw a 6 - 8 point buck standing about 50 yards away looking at us.  We both scrambled for our rifles and took different paths around our parked vehicles to try to get a shot off at the buck.  I manage to get one shot off and when I pull the trigger a second time nothing happened.  I look down at my rifle and see that the bullet clip has dropped out onto the ground.  At this point, the buck is probably 200 yards away and just before it gets to the woods another hunter fires three or four shots at it.  

Nancy comes out with hot beef sandwiches and other food.  During lunch we talked about what deer drives we would make in the afternoon.  I think we made three drives in rifle: 1) across the creek, 2) the cedars and 3) east of the cedars all the way to the logging road next to Oscar's property.  After the last drive we decided to go over to shotgun for one more drive.

This drive in shotgun is the one where I shot a 5-point buck back in 1992.  I get to be a driver on this one and get the lane that goes along the edge of the field where it ends in some really thick stuff.  As I barely get started I hear a flurry of shots.   Just before I get out I hear a deer get up and then hear some type of metallic sound. When I get out I talk to Steve who was standing in the field near the corner where I kicked up the deer.  Steve said the deer was a huge doe that tried to jump the fence but did not clear it.  For safety reasons, Steve did not shoot.

Steve and I then go down the field to the back corner where a number of hunters are gathered.  When I get there, I find Jamie gutting out a big doe.  Apparently another hunter got shooting at a doe and Dennis had a quick view of a doe or buck but did not get a shot.  When all is done, the results for the day are: two bucks and two does.  We go to Rock Falls to register the deer and back to the farm for the night.
