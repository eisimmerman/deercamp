Neilsville Wisconsin

1978 - 1983 -  p.2.

Learning to Hunt on My Own

On another hunt I was sitting on Buzz’ stand, on a folding chair on the ground.  It had snowed before season and at dawn it was very still and somewhat crunchy and around 7 a.m. I could hear the soft crunch of a deer’s hooves in the snow.  As I slowly turned around I could see a buck with its head down and feeding.  It was near a blow down and I put my scope on an open spot where I thought the buck would have to walk around the blow down.  As it did, I shot and dropped the buck at about 30 yards.  Gene was nearby on John’s stand and upon hearing the shot came over.  I remember shaking nervously after shooting the buck and when Gene came over, pulled out my copy of how to field dress a deer that I had copied out of Outdoor Life or Field and Stream magazine.  Gene read off the steps to me as I gutted the deer.  It took about forty-five minutes, being one of the first deer I had gutted myself.

My biggest buck, rack wise and body wise, was a eight pointer that I shot off dad’s stand.  It should have been a nine pointer but had one tine that either was broken off or not developed.  This buck was the buck that “almost wasn’t”.  For a few years I had heard and seen deer running on the north side of the swamp, but they were always just out of sight and shooting range.  Well in pre-season scouting I had walked across the swamp and up on top of the ridge only to find a permanent deer stand that was made out of sticks and small logs that were “woven” together to form a sort of blind.  You could stand in the center of it and it came up almost chest high.  So you could look around in all directions without much body movement seen and had a rest for your gun.  I though it looked abandoned, so I decided I would go there on opening day.

On opening day I arrived at the blind about 6:30 a.m.  I decided to set up in front of the blind and heard a loud crunching sound that I was convinced was a deer.  It kept getting closer and closer until finally this senior citizen of a hunter arrived and told me I was sitting on his stand.  He said he had been hunting this land for a long time.  I said this was public land and that I had been hunting this area since 1971.  Finally after a short standoff, I relented and went back over the swamp to dad’s stand.
