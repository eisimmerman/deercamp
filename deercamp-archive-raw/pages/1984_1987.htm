Neilsville Wisconsin

1984 - 1987

The Clear Cut Years in the Clark County Forest

Gene Melville, Paul and Allen had been to Neilsville scouting and reported back that the county forest where we hunted in Unit 55 had been largely clear-cut by a lumber company.  The woods had been wiped out pretty much from the beaver dam to just within 25 yards of Buzz’s stand by the fallen horizontal birch tree.  Now instead of having the long walk in from the cemetery we had a shorter walk in from the railroad tracks, which was good since walking through all the clear-cut debris was hard.  Now we let Paul lead the way in to the stands in the morning since he was more familiar with coming in from that direction.

Since Buzz’s old stand was now very close to the edges of the clear cut, we began to hunt by using Dad’s stand and Hal’s old stand which was by an old stump that was just up on the other side of the draw, about 100 yards from Dad’s stand.  After a few years, we moved Hal’s stand about 50 yards south in order to see over a ridge that blocked view to deer trails that we could always “hear deer on” but not see them.

One hunt Gene was on Dad’s stand and I was sitting on the new stand.  As usual, it was before 9 a.m. on opening day when I heard a deer running my direction.  When I turned to look for it, it must have noticed my movement as it stopped.  Then it took off again running to the southwest.  All the time while it was running it was snorting very loudly, must have done it six or more times.  I saw it was a buck and waited for an open shot that probably presented itself but I could not seem to “squeeze off” the trigger and just let it run off.  After it was gone, I was shaking really hard and realized that I had just had my first case of “buck fever”.  Later, both Gene Melville and Paul Melville stated they had heard the loud snorting of the buck.
