Pine City, MN

2003 - p1.

Minnesota and Wisconsin Deer Seasons

November 7 - MN Pre-Opener Stand Setup

This year I had the good fortune to hunt with Steve Lauer, a guy I became friends with when I worked for a company called DaVita.  Steve had a farm and private land just east of Pine City and said he would have a place where I could hunt .  So I drove the 90 miles up to his farm, met him and we walked out about a mile or more to place my portable tree stand up 25 feet in a huge pine tree on an island next to a beaver dam.  Steve said that anyone who sits and waits long enough in this stand always sees deer.  Given the location is 90 miles or 90 minutes away from home in Burnsville, Steve suggests I get up at 2:30 a.m. to leave by 3:00 a.m. in order to get to the walk-in point by 4:30 a.m.  I'll be hunting on public land next to Steve's private land, so he expects there will be a lot of hunting pressure.

November 8 - The Opener

I end up being the second hunter there at that time and it takes me about 30 minutes to walk in.  At 5:00 p.m. I am sitting on the ground at the bottom of my tree and decide to wait an hour to 6 a.m. before climbing into the tree stand.  Now 30 minutes before legal shooting time, I wait the final moments before first light.  As dawn comes, I am sure I hear deer coming my way from several directions, however, nothing comes into view.  So....7 a.m. becomes 8 a.m. which creeps closer to 9 a.m.  At about 9 a.m. I am looking straight out in front of me and when I turn and look to the right, I see a doe standing on the frozen water next to a beaver lodge.  I swivel my body around and attempt a shot to the right.  I'm concerned because of the awkward angle and also that my arthritis-weakened shoulder may not be able to hold up the rifle steady for a long-range shot.
