Drummond Wisconsin

1990 p2.

Bayfield County, off Porcupine Trail

Opening Day Saturday November 17

First new hunting location for the opener since 1971.  End of the day results:  Gene S, Eric, Allen, and Fred – 0 deer seen.  Gene M. saw two does about 8 a.m. with one whose stomach “swung from side-to-side” when she walked.  Gene M. had picked out a stand behind the shack that overlooked the ravine on which the shack sits.  Tally – two deer, no shots.  This was quite a shock to all of us since we were used to seeing all the deer and hearing all the shots when we hunted around Neilsville.

Sunday November 18

Day 2.  Arrived at my stand at 6 a.m., and put out several 35mm canisters of doe-in-heat lure.  I climbed up into my tree stand and was pulling my rifle up to me on the safety rope when I heard several deer run into the scent canisters.  I had not had a chance to put a bullet into the chamber yet and I couldn’t move since they were so close.  As it got lighter, I could start to make out some forms and shapes.  I wasn’t sure how long they would hang around so I tried to pull back the action on the rifle slowly, but the metallic click was so loud that the deer all ran off.  After I thought all was lost, I heard one deer slowing walking in the brush and leaves.  Closer and closer it came until I was sure I could see it any minute.  Then, it slowly walked away and never did show itself.  Very warm – 40+ degrees.

Thanksgiving November 22

10:30 a.m.  I ran into Fred coming out of the woods as I was walking in to go move my tree stand.  Fred was going out for lunch.  Allen and Fred would hunt Thursday and Friday, and then Fred would head back to Wausau.  About three inches of snow on the ground.
