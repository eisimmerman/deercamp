Wisconsin and Minnesota Deer Seasons

November 5 - Minnesota Stand Placement

Like last year, I again hunt with my friend Steve Lauer up by his farm east of Pine City, MN.   I meet Steve at his farm and we walk the mile or so in on the logging trail and out to the big pine tree on the island in the swamp.  Like last year, Steve offers to put the stand up for me, about 25 - 30 feet up.  We lock the stand in place and head back out.  Steve and Mark head off toward Steve's brother's stand to hang up some type of trick/gag to greet him when he gets to his stand on opening morning.  I finish walking out and drive the 90 miles/minutes back to Apple Valley.

November 6 - Minnesota Opener

Because of the 90 mile/90 minute drive, I get up at 3:30 a.m. to leave by 4:00 a.m. with a target of getting to the park and walk-in spot by 5:30 a.m.  That should allow me 30 minutes to walk in and get to my stand by 6:00 a.m. in time for legal shooting hours starting at 6:30 a.m.  It has been quite rainy in November so there are quite a few low-lying areas filled with water.  Anyway, I'm ready to hunt by 6:15 a.m. Like last year, I hear lots of sounds that suggest deer are headed my way, but over the course of 10 hours this day I do not see a deer.  The temp is somewhere in the mid-50's and I hunt most of the day without any gloves on.  Any shooting I do hear seems to be far away and lots of it in the direction of where Steve and others should be hunting.  

Around 4:30 p.m. I get the itch to call it a day and start walking out.  As I cross the bridge over the river, I notice a beaver swimming and watch it for some time.  After about five minutes, I notice Mark pulling a deer behind him so I wait for him to meet me on the bridge.  Mark tells me he shot the doe at about 9 a.m. and then shot a big buck at 3:00 p.m.  The buck was too big for him to drag by himself, so he would go back later or drag it out Sunday.  I help Mark put the doe in the back of my SUV and drive down to the farm.  After a pop and some beef sticks, I head home.
