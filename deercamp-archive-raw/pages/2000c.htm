Abbotsford, WI

2000 - p.4.

Year 7 - Larry Meyer's Land

November 18 – WI Deer Gun Opener

Apparently I had hit the deer (by now I know it is a doe) in the fleshly part between the backbone or spine and the hide, about 2 inches worth.  So the shot knocked it down but did not kill the deer.  As it ran away I could see a dark brown spot where I had hit it.  So, just like the 1999 season, if you don’t make the first shot count, trying to make any follow-up shots count with a bolt action rifle is hopeless, at least in WI where the deer are usually pressured and the chance of getting a standing or walking shot does not happen often. 

Sometime during the morning, a hunter came through on a deer drive and stopped right out in front of me.  He asked if I had permission to hunt on the land and I told him I did.  I told him that I was hunting on land that belonged to Larry Meyer and I was part of the hunting party with Duane Meyer.  I then related the story from 2 years ago when Gene and I were hunting back here and some other hunter who pretended that he was Larry Meyer told Gene that he had to get off the land.  I said that problem had been corrected and that I and my friend Lorin were ok to hunt here.  The hunter then explained that the land on the other side of the fence corner, to the east and south was his land and that I had better not shoot at any deer on his land.  I said ok and he finally walked off.

At about 11:30 am I get down to walk to Lorin’s stand for lunch.  He tells me he has seen 8 deer, all does.  Lorin is hunting WI for the first time in 23 years, so he is holding out for a buck on the first day.  We walk out to Lorin’s truck to warm up and eat lunch and then head back into the woods at 1 pm for the afternoon hunt.  As we walk out for lunch, I notice that most of the bright eyes tacks I had marked our path on the trees had been taken down.  Probably was the guy who had stopped by to give me a hard time.
