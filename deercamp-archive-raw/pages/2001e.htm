Pre-season Scouting

November 10 - School Forest Area

seems awfully high, but since all that I have read that hunters sitting up 20 feet high or more are detected much less often, I decide to give it a go.  Just by the time I finish up, Dan comes back after taking walk around the property with his shotgun looking for grouse.  Dan replies that both the grouse and deer sign are scarce, with the best deer sign right out in front of my stand.  So Dan and I go about 150 yards down the logging trail and pick out a good tree for Lorin that provides enough distance between the two of us but should cover the trails in between.

We mark the tree so that Lorin and I can later find it and then I leave my "bright eyes" tacks on some trees to mark my trail out to the road.  When we get out we find a note from Gene stating that he and Duane were heading back to the school forest since there was only one good stand site on the new land and not two as would be needed for him and Ben.  So, once three I find Gene and Duane's trucks parked by where I had my stand in the school forest in 1998.  Duane walks in only to find a bow hunter not far from where they want to put Ben's stand, but he backs out in respect of the hunter.  We decide to put Gene's stand up instead, at a spot called the "well", a sink hole that a boy scout troop had framed with rocks years ago, which looked like the opening to a water well.  We put up Gene's stand on a tree that covers several trails.  While I mark the trails with tape for visual signposts of their location, Gene sets up his safety/shooting rail that I gave him for his birthday.

In addition to this being a safety device, it provides a "rock solid" shooting rest when needed.  After finishing there, Duane and Dan decide to go back bow hunting.  They agree that they will come back
