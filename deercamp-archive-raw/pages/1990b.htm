Drummond Wisconsin

1990 p3.

Bayfield County, off Porcupine Trail

Friday November 23

A big doe and fawn came out to Fred and he shot both of them with his 7MM.  First deer taken up here for this hunting party. 

Saturday, Nov. 24-Last Weekend

Gene and Eric arrived Friday night.  Allen and a friend from work, Brian, arrived as well.  Saturday zero deer were seen.  We decided to drive into Drummond for a beer at the “pissing bear”.  Then we drove up the road to the Chequamegon bar.  One guy kept asking me a lot of questions about playing pool.  He wasn’t too interested in challenging me after I told him I only played bumper pool or would shoot the eight ball straight in.  Gene and Allen were trying very hard not to break down from busting a gut laughing while I maintained a straight face.  About three to six inches of snow on the ground. 

Sunday, Nov. 25-Last Weekend

Cold.  Very cold.  Radio said three degrees below zero at 6:00 a.m.  Gene and I walked out to our stands behind the shack.  I sat on Gene M.’s stand and Gene S. went in further north.  About 9 a.m. I heard a shot south of the shack across the road.  Apparently Allen saw the trail of a deer and tracked it until he got a shot at it, about 80 yards.  The doe ran about 30 yards before dropping – lung shot, lots of blood.  Allen and Brian met Gene and I at the shack.  We walked in south of Porcupine trail until we found the doe.  Allen gutted it out and Gene and I took turns dragging it out.  We broke camp around 2 p.m.
