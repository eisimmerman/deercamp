Abbotsford, WI

1996 - p.1.

Back to the School Forest

Saturday, November 16, Pre-Season Scouting

I bring two portable tree stands along with me this year so that I have another stand to hunt from if one is cold.  I hang my first portable near where Gene had his ladder stand last year near the creek.  Then I go back to base camp and pick up the second stand.

Based on this fall’s bow hunting as well as observations from fall 1995, Duane and Dan decide that they will have me set up my ladder stand across the swamp and up over the ridge from where they traditionally had set up stands in the past.  As this is the first year after the clear cut, the new growth in the forest comes in very thick.  As we walk north from base camp, we walk over temporary logging roads that the lumber trucks have rutted into the ground.  Lots of stumps and slashings lying around.  We find a spot where I hang up one of my homemade portable tree stands.  I use the skyhook tree steps, which hang on bolts that go into the tree.  That way you can take the steps down each hunt to prevent anyone from climbing up into your stand or tampering with it.

Gene has his ladder stand put up on the south side of the school forest, actually on some private land that we get permission to hunt on.  Gene sets up his stand just east of where Duane’s stand is. 

For me, both stands look good and I choose to hunt near the creek on opening day.

Saturday, November 23  –The Opener

The opener is very quiet with little shooting by me.  I see no deer at all in the morning.  We have now begun to meet for lunch over by the private land that Dave and Duane are now hunting on across from the school forest.  We all report that the morning was slow and hope for a better afternoon hunt.  The rest of the day is like the first half, with no deer taken that day

Sunday, November 24 – Day 2 

I decide to hunt off my homemade portable stand on Sunday.  The morning is again quiet until around 9 a.m. when I look to my left into a thick patch of brush and see a dark horizontal form.  I can’t tell if it is a buck or doe, but it is a big-bodied deer. It stands for at least five minutes.  I watch it closely and see it start to slowly move out toward an overgrown logging road.  I put the scope of my Remington 7MM on a small opening between two tall birch trees and pull the trigger when I see part of the deer’s chest come into view.  The deer drops instantly!  I climb down from my tree stand and pace off the distance to the deer, which comes to 120 yards!  When I get to the deer and since I don’t see horns sticking out above the ears I assume it is a doe.  However, when I pick up the deer’s head I find the deer is really a buck that has both horns broke off at about one inch off the head.  So I had to register it as a doe since it does not have at least one antler three inches or longer.
