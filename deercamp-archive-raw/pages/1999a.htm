Year 6 - New Land

November 20 - The Opener 

Gene and I wake up at 4:30 a.m. in Colby and hit the road by 5:15 a.m. and get to the new hunting land before 6 a.m.  We take our time getting ready at the car because it is still quite dark out.  Finally, we start walking back in…the bright eyes tacks clearly pointing out our direction.  We get to Gene’s first stand on the logging road about 15 minutes later.  Gene decides to hunt here first and then if the action is slow consider moving to the stand back farther in later.  I continue down the trail to reach my stand and get up, put on the safety belt and am ready to hunt before 6:30 a.m. 

At about 7 a.m. I am looking north from my tree stand and scan around to the right and notice some movement.  I swivel around and see a deer that is walking slowly from the southeast to the northeast.  I look harder and am able to pick out a set of horns on the deer that are very light color.  I position my rifle against the tree and hold on an opening in the brush between some trees.  Because it is early yet and the light level is low it is hard to find the buck in the scope.  So, for a moment I have to lift my head up to find the deer again, pick out another spot to shoot through and fire when I think the deer has come into my field of view. 

The buck turns around right away and instead of running off, slowly starts walking back to the southeast.  I manage to get off three more shots but with no luck.  Looking back now, I realize that I did not position the stand to allow me to swivel around to get a comfortable shot in that direction.  Well, I was quite disappointed at missing the shots, but I tried to gather myself and get ready to hunt again.  Around 8 a.m. I noticed two does coming my way from the northeast.  I waited for them to get behind some cover and then readied my gun.  The lead doe was the biggest and when she stepped out in front of me into a clearing 50 yards away, I aimed, shot and missed!  The doe jumped back in the brush so I reloaded and shot again when it stepped out…and missed again!!
