Drummond Wisconsin

1992 -  p.4.

Year 3 Bayfield County

Nov. 20  - Day before the Opener

It's 3:30 p.m. now and the rain has stopped, however a fog has rolled in.  Someone named Bertrand stopped by at 2:30 p.m. and wanted to know if we wanted him to plow the road from Johnnie's corner down to the shack.  I told him no because I was able to make the drive with my front-wheel car and the others would have 4-wheel drive.

Gene and Allen arrived at 6 p.m.  They got started late from Wausau (Gene says Allen did not have his gear packed!).  We played a hunting and fishing trivia game until about 9 p.m.  Fred was the big winner answering 3 of 5 categories correctly.

Nov. 21  - The Opener

As usual, I’m the first one up when the alarm goes off at 4:30 a.m. on Saturday.  Fred gets ready quickly as he has the longest drive to Clam Lake.  I leave with him as the front-wheel drive Nissan Sentra I am driving might have trouble getting through the snow.  I do get stuck on one hill going out Porcupine trail to Highway D, so Fred backs his Ford Explorer up to me and hooks up a towing chain and pulls me out to where the road levels out and is plowed. 

I drive out and manage to find the bright eyes tacks I had placed near the road so that I could find the logging trail I would use on my walk in to the stand.  I notice that someone has tampered with the tacks, but I am able to find my way as even though snow has fallen, the land looks somewhat familiar.  On the way in I see a few deer tracks that show that deer have already moved through the area.  I make it onto the swamp island and climb my way up into the tree stand.  Like last year, I am amazed at how quiet the opening morning is compared to the “deer wars” that we used to experience down in Neilsville.
