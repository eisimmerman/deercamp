Abbotsford, WI

2001 - p.12.

November 18 - Day Two

He comes down and we talk about the "boom".  Apparently, this was a sonic boom/backfire from a low-flying jet that was on a training mission.  Well, we laughed and talked about this some more and then agreed to hunt until 10 a.m. and then drive back to the school forest to meet the gang for lunch and listen to the Packer game.  Duane decided to go to his land up North to hunt a big buck that lives on the property, so we did not have the usual grilled venison to eat for lunch.  Near the end of lunch it begins to rain hard, so Gene and I agree to the plan to go to Larry's farm to see if we can get into some deer drives.  The rain really starts coming down harder while we watch the final minutes of the Packer game on TV in Larry's house.  Larry helps to hang my buck up in his barn and cuts the tenderloins out for Lorin and I to fry up and eat.  Since it looks like hunting is done for the day, Lorin and I drive back to Larry Meyer's land and take down his stand.  We go out to eat and then Lorin takes off for Madison on Monday morning.

November 19 - Day Three

Today the plan is to go take down my tree stand and then go to Larry's farm to do some deer drives, skin and cut up my buck.  Since I found out I left the keys to my tree stand chain lock back in Minneapolis, I end up going to a hardware store in Abbotsford to get a bolt cutter to cut off the cable locks that secure the stand to the tree.  It takes about an hour to get everything down and packed in the car and back on the road. I drive down to Larry's farm and am greeted by Gene who comes out to tell me that he has shot a doe on one of the morning deer drives.  

As Gene tells the story, he was a stander on the drive and six does came out to him and stood only about 30 yards away from him.  Gene thought about it and then decided to shoot the biggest doe.  It ran off after he shot.  Larry's son Craig came out off the drive and when Gene told him he shot, they went to look for the deer.  The deer had bedded down and when hearing the hunters, got up at which point Craig shot it and dropped it for good.  


Eric and Ben
