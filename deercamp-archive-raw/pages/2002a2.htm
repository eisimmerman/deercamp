Abbotsford, WI

2002 - p8.

Gene and Ben Simmerman

Opening Day - Abbotsford, WI

Well, it's now 7:17 a.m. and Gene has just fired four shots at two bucks.  Neither one went more than 35 yards before disappearing so he figured that was a good sign.  Gene got down from his stand and walked north onto the island where he instantly found blood and corn on the ground.  So the trail got bloodier as it winded into swampy area.  He turned a corner West and there it was.  After taking some pictures he dragged it back across the beaver dam to his stand.  Well, it's now 8 a.m. so he decided to go look for the other buck.  He went straight to where he shot, didn't see any blood so headed West, then North following its tracks.  When he cut North after 30 yards he found the second buck...a 7 pointer!  I dragged the buck back across the beaver dam and took this picture of the two bucks together.

Finally back in his stand at 9:30 a.m. to hunt.  Gene got down at noon to go get Ben and his buck tag.  After driving back to the turnaround we parked and ate lunch.  We first  drug out the 7-pointer and got it out to the truck at 2 p.m.  He then took Ben back to  his stand for the afternoon hunt.  Gene went back to his stand and started dragging the fork-horn out.  When he was about 50 yards from being out to his truck he ran into another hunter, Rod Radke - who is Don Meyer's son-in-law.  He helped drag the buck out to Gene's truck and lift it in.  Gene went back to his stand for the rest of the afternoon hunt and then walked out at dark to go pick up Ben who was waiting on the road.
