Drummond Wisconsin

1990

Bayfield County, off Porcupine Trail

The Melville’s were co-owners on 40 acres of land in the Chequamegon National Forest near Cable/Drummond WI.  They had built a small hunting shack on the property which usually was empty during the opening weekend of deer hunting season, so Allen decided he wanted to try hunting up there since Neilsville was no longer good hunting.  This was the “big woods” of the North, lots of mature hardwoods with few farm fields/crops so patterning the deer was going to be a challenge. 

October 27 Scouting

First trip to the deer shack, between Cable and Drummond, near Lake Owen in southern Bayfield County.  Located about three miles off county road D, on Porcupine trail. We talked to the local caretaker, “Johnnie” who said there were some big deer down by Club Lake, east of D.  Allen and Gene Melville, Gene S. and Eric rode with Fred Spindler.  We saw a couple of deer in the woods from the road.  All of us took a walk down a logging trail south of Club Lake road.  I found a big aspen tree west of two small dried up swamps that looked over a wooded draw and ridge.  We hung up my portable tree stand that I had bought this year and would be my first experience hunting off the ground.  Allen and Gene S. took off to find a stand and ended up a couple hundred yards west of me, overlooking a swamp.  By the time they returned it was almost dark, so Allen and Fred would have to return next weekend to scout.

Opening Weekend

Everyone arrived in camp in the evening.  Gene Melville and Jerry Selling were already there and “in-the-bag”.  Gene M. told a story about how he and a friend named Delmer once drove back and forth on Porcupine trail between Lake Owen (county 213) and highway D.  Apparently it took until 4 a.m. before they found the shack.
