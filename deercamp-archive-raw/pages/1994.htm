Abbotsford, WI

1994

A New Place to Hunt

I only hunted for two half-days that fall.  There was one memorable event that did not involve shooting that I'll describe in a moment.

Because the kids were small and Cathy needed help with them, I agreed to let the family come along to my brother Gene's in Wausau.  I would hunt in the mornings and help out with the kids in the afternoon while Gene hunted.  Gene had met a guy (Duane) who liked to hunt deer and offered to let him and I hunt with him in the school forest near Colby, WI.

On one of the morning hunts, I heard a deer walk through very close to me, but it was too dark to see the animal.  It finally walked out of hearing distance and as it got lighter another hunter who had just come in at daylight and sat right on the logging road had the deer walk right in front of him.  It turned out to be a doe.  

The second half-day was an experience I won't forget.  A local hunting party was making a drive across the road from the Colby school forest and I heard what I thought was a herd of deer coming my way.  The noise of crunching brush was the loudest I had ever heard while hunting.  Man was I excited!!

I kept my eyes on an opening where I thought the deer would come from.  After what seemed like minutes, I saw the brush and small trees swaying back and forth....and then out popped a black bear!  He looked around and then continued on a path off to my left.  He was gone from sight in about 30 seconds.

Fortunately, Gene had success that day.  Apparently a buck-fawn deer walked by Gene's tree stand close enough so that Gene fired one shot from his 243 rifle and dropped it.  There was no snow on the ground at this time which made the drag out more difficult.  So, my hunting season was short, but memorable!
