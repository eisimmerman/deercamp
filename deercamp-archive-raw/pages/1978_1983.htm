Neilsville Wisconsin

1978 - 1983 - p.1.

Learning to Hunt on My Own

During this five-year period I had both good and bad luck.  I had inherited my dad’s 30-06 rifle, which he had bought in 1968 and it had begun to give me jamming problems.  I could usually get off one shot, but then the action froze partly open as it was trying to load another shell.  This resulted in some missed opportunities at bucks.  One time I was on dad’s stand facing west.  Some shots were fired and I heard a deer running my direction.  Since it was running directly at me I could see that it was a spike buck.  I got off a shot at it that missed, then the buck stopped and looked back at me.  I put the buck in the scope again and pulled the trigger and when nothing happened, dropped the gun down to look at it.  The action had ejected the shell and had jammed open.  Well, the buck ran towards Gene and I heard him pop off two or three shells from the Remington 12-gauge semi-auto shotgun.  He did not touch it either.

Another time while on dad’s stand I had a few deer run out of the swamp from North to South.  I picked out the largest one and as it was running I lead it and touched off the shot.  As the deer started to slow down I threw my gun up again only to find it had jammed open.  In the next moment the deer fell to the ground   I walked over to it and found it was a yearling buck fawn that must have been born early since it was bigger than the typical fawn.
