Pine City, MN

2003 - p2.

Minnesota Deer Season First

November 8 - The MN Opener

I take a shot and see that it hits the doe in one of the lower front legs.  I see it tries to limp off the ice and I get another shot at it.  It still doesn't drop so I shoot a third time.  It tries to go North back into the woods and disappears into the tall swamp grass.  I look down at my gun and see that it has jammed open.  I free up the jam and decide to get down from my stand and go find where the doe disappeared to.

It's a long walk over to where the doe was last seen, but I get to the beaver lodge and then walk off to the right in the tall swamp grass where I last saw it.  After a few more yards I find the doe dead in the grass.  It's a nice size doe.  Now I remember that I forgot my tag and knife to gut out the deer.

On the way back to the tree stand I begin to walk off or pace off the distance to measure how far the shot was.  When I finally get to the tree, it ends up being about 150 yards!  So I climb back up to get my pack and back down to go back to the deer.  The swamp grass is very tall and thick and with some light snow on the ground, it is hard walking back to the doe.  Of courser it will be even harder dragging the doe back to the tree.  After gutting out the doe and dragging it back, it ends up being about 10:30 a.m.  I hunt for one more hour and the get down to go find a hunter in the party who is supposed to be at the opposite end of the beaver pond.  There should be a deer cart there which would be very helpful to get the deer out.  Otherwise, I will have to put the doe on the plastic deer sled and leave early to get the doe out before dark.

As I am not exactly sure where the other hunter is I give up looking for him after about 20 minutes.  So I get back in my tree stand and hunt to about 4:15 p.m.  At 4:30 p.m. I am ready to go 


150 doe shot!
