Year 7 - Larry Meyer's Land

November 11, Pre-season Scouting

We get up, unhurt (except our pride!) and wonder what to do about the tree stand, which is now bent like a right angle.  We turn over the ladder and stand on it to straighten it out and then turn the bent section around so it can still be used.  Finally, after about another 45 minutes and half a dozen tie downs, the stand is up and pretty stable.  We make our way back to the school forest and while Duane and Dan get their afternoon bow hunting stands ready, we all go down the road to find an empty spot to shoot our rifles.  Gene is shooting Dad’s 1968 Remington Model 742 30-06 autoloader.  Ben is shooting a 20-gauge shotgun with slugs.  Lorin is shooting a 338 Remington and I shoot a 7MM magnum.  Gene and Ben leave for Colby and then Wausau .  Lorin and I spend the night at the Abby Inn in Abbotsford.  Sunday morning, we leave early for home.  The season opener is set for Saturday, November 18. 

November 17 – Day before the Opener

I arrived Thursday night the 16th so as to beat the Friday traffic and be able to check out the deer stands Friday a.m. to ensure they were not tampered with.  I drive about 20 minutes to get there.  There is about 3-4 inches of snow in the woods, perfect for spotting deer.  The wind is up, but lighter in the woods.  There are lots of fresh tracks near Lorin’s stand.  Heading back to my stand, I notice that a deer had walked straight down the logging trail, with drag marks between the tracks so it may have been a buck.  My stand is fine and I notice that someone had walked in from the backside of the wood lot and walked in the direction of the other stand that is about 100 yards away from me.  It takes about 20 minutes for me to walk out of the woods and get to the RAV4.  Back to the hotel to prepare and wait for the opener tomorrow.
