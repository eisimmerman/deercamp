November 10 - School Forest Scouting

As has been typical of other scouting trips, I rose early and left Minneapolis by 6:30 a.m. for the 2.5 hour drive over to WI.  The routine has been to meet my brother Gene (and son Ben too now) and Duane and Dan over by the school forest where we meet for lunch.  I always look forward to this drive as it really is the "official" kickoff for the fall deer hunting season.  There is as much excitement and importance to this trip as the actual haunt itself.  The ability to read the deer sign in your hunting area, and based on that sign pick the most likely stand spot to hunt from is probably the single most important factor that impacts your hunting success..   The majority of hunting stand locations don't change from year to hear, but as our extended hunting gang all hunt in a 5-mile radius from the school forest with most of us on private land, something changes year to year that requires a search for a new stand.  This year Gene and Ben had to look for a new stand since the owner of the woods where they hunted last year had built a house and did not want hunters on the property this year.
