Wisconsin Deer Season

November 7 - Elk Mound Scouting

about 2 hours!  The good thing is that Phil can now see across the creek into the dry swamp and beyond up to the ridge, so his field of view/hunting has expanded considerably.  

Now the next thing is to find a new location for my stand.  We go back to a general area that Phil had picked out a couple of weeks earlier when the kids and I drove over to help Phil put up some firewood for the winter.  After getting there, we look closer for more deer sign and find at least 4 different trails running through the area, with many of them crossing to create a nice trail hub.  We place orange "tape flags" at the trail crossings so that we can step back and visually see where they are in order to pick a good stand spot that can cover them.  After a few minutes more we end up picking a tree that is where Phil's dad used to have a stand.  It's on a nice little mound that is slightly raised and covers the trail hubs nicely.

My stand goes up pretty fast with the only challenge being to get the tie-down straps secure.  On the way out to the meadow I put out my bright eyes tacks and Phil helps clear a stand trail.  Overall the sign looks great as there as many heavily used trails to watch.  I'm pumped!

Next we go down to Dennis' stand along the creek and just clean it up and also put a few more branches around it to break up the outline of the hunter.  Finally, we go across the creek and also clean up/fix a stand just up off the bank of the creek.  The stand is the same one that I was at last year (2003) when I shot the buck fawn.

So...a long but productive day in the woods, think we were done around 4 p.m.  Claire and I picked up food at McDonalds and headed back to the twin cities.  Less than two weeks to go to the Wisconsin opener!!
