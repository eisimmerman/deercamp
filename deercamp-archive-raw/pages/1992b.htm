Drummond Wisconsin

1992 -  p.3.

Year 3 Bayfield County

Oct. 24  - Pre-Season Scouting

Gene and I drive to Cable to get a refill on the LP gas tank.  At noon, we go behind the shack to Eric's new stand which we decide that Gene should use this Fall.  We brush out shooting lanes and set up his ladder stand.  We drop about 50 pounds of corn there and another 50 pound of corn at the trail hub where we dropped the salt in the spring.    

Arriving back at the shack we find a couple of trees that will work for putting up a meat pole.  We try out the setup and find I am able to walk on the 4x4 12' timber that is fixed to the trees with chain.  We agree it should be able to hang from 6 - 8 deer from it.  We then drive down Porcupine to find a clearing at which to shoot off 2.5 boxes of shotgun shells at clay pigeons.  After supper we went to a supper club called Pla-Mor bar on Lake Namekagon.  We watched the baseball world series for a while and then went up the road to the Pioneer Bar.  Later, we ended up at the Triangle Bar and then got back to the shack at 11:30 p.m.

Oct. 25  - Pre-Season Scouting

Breakfast, cleanup and break camp by 10 a.m.  Looking forward to the opener on 11/21/92.  

Nov. 20  - Day before the Opener

I'm the first one at camp, having arrived at 12:15 p.m.  The drive down porcupine trail was better than I expected.  I unloaded the car, shoveled out the snow to make some parking space, hung the meat pole up and brought some wood into the shack.  I quickly unpacked my gear inside.  It is about 32 degrees with light rain.  There appears to be about 3 inches of snow on the ground.  The forecast is for continued rain/sleet tonight with more on Saturday.  Good thing I'll be protected from the elements up in the pine tree.  I expect Fred will arrive next followed by Gene and then Allen.
