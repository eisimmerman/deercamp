Abbotsford, WI

1995 - p.4.

Year 2 - Hunting Near the School Forest

Saturday, November 18 - The Opener

To get me set up, I went with Joe to sit in a tree stand next to a willow swamp.  This was on the way to Joe's stand so he could drop me off.  On the way there we came to a snowmobile trail which if you went left you would go to Duane's stand and if you went right you would go North out of the school forest.  It was very windy and cold in the afternoon so I came back with Joe when he came down from his stand at 4:30 p.m. 

Sunday, November 19 - Day 2

Gene sat in Dan's ladder stand which was northeast from base camp.  Apparently a buck came by at about 90 yards and Gene missed a shot at it with his 243 because the bullet hit some trees in front of the buck.  Later, Gene shot again through some small trees and gut-shot a doe that ran off.  Joe and Dave chased it through the school forest and across county line road.  The doe laid down by a creek and after lunch they kicked it up again.  Duane nailed it with the "coup de grace" killing shot.  Finally Duane talked Gene through the process to gut the deer out.

Monday, November 20 - Day 3

For today's hunt Duane put me back on his stand which is farther southwest from base camp and nestled between thick swamps on two sides and a ridge on a third or west side making for a natural funnel.  Whoever sat here often saw lots of deer
