Abbotsford, WI

1999 - p.1

Year 6 - New Land

November 13 – Pre-Season Scouting

Duane tells Gene and I that he has gotten permission to hunt on a 40-acre lot a few miles away from the school forest.  He thinks that would be a good move for us as with less woods left from the clear cutting, there are fewer places to put up a stand and more hunters in the same area.  Duane had done some earlier scouting of the new area during bow hunting season and had found at least 3 good locations for stands.  Two of them are just off a logging trail and the third is back in farther.  We go drop one of Gene’s ladder stands by the first location.  It looks good with many trails coming together near it.  Then we go down the trail farther to the fence line and drop my stand by a tree that covers both the fence line and a few trails that cross the logging road.  The stand will face north to north-west and have a nice pine tree in back of me for screening cover. 

Gene, Duane and I then go and drop the third ladder stand in the far back corner.  This stand looks the best in terms of sign, good trails, a few rubs, etc.  While Gene and I then go about putting up the stands, Duane and his son Dan go off to do more scouting.  After setting up this stand, Gene and I hang bright eyes tacks on the trail back out to the logging road. We all then go back down the road to find an abandoned logging road to set up our target to sight in our deer hunting rifles.  Everyone gets their shots on target and we feel good about how the guns are shooting as well as the stand locations we have set up.

In late afternoon, Duane and Dan head off to go bow hunting.  Gene and I go back to Colby, WI to pick up his wife and we head back to Wausau, WI to relax for the night with a few beers and watch the movie “Animal House”.
