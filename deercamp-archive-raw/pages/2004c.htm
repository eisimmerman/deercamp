Elk Mound, WI

2004 - p4.

Wisconsin Deer Season

November 19 - Day Before The Opener

I took the day off from work with the intention of driving over to Elk Mound is the early afternoon.  Heavy rain has come in again and it looks like it will be a wet one today.  After hours of running around, Christopher and I leave at about 4:30 p.m.  We grab some McDonalds on the way out and finally get to Phil's house around 7 p.m.  After only being there about 30 minutes I find out that not only have I left Christopher's warm clothing back in the twin cities, but I also have left my license too!  As much as I don't want to drive there and back, I have to and tell Phil I expect to be back around 10 p.m.

The round-trip 180 mile drive fortunately was uneventful.  Now back in Elk Mound I get my gear ready and go to bed about 11 p.m.  We agreed to get up at 4:30 a.m. on the opening morning just so we had enough time for everything.

November 20 - The Opener

As is typical on the morning of the opening hunt for deer I always wake up before the alarm goes off.  The anticipation always gets the best of me and the sleep the night before is usually not long nor completely restful.  After breakfast and getting dressed, we drive out to rifle country and get there at about 5:30 a.m.  Phil tells me he has left a trail of corn stalks and left-over debris from the crop harvest in the field.  He says that the trail leads directly over to where we should enter the woods in order to pick up the bright eyes tacks that lead us to our stand.

As Christopher and I get started, we find the trail of corn debris on the field and head out.  After about 50 yards, I hear a whistle from Phil and turn around to find him walking towards us.  Apparently, I mistakenly followed some waste corn debris that veered slightly off to the left and was headed in the wrong direction.  Given the total darkness at this time of the day I was not surprised at this.  However, when daylight was up later, it is very visible how the trail runs straight across to the woods.
