Neilsville, Wisconsin

1971 - p.1.
 The Neilsville, WI Years

I don’t remember much about my first year of deer hunting.  I do recall that blaze orange clothing was not yet required.  My brother Gene and I wore red cotton sweat pants and jackets that the wind blew right through and we spent more time thinking about how to stay warm or when we would get out of the woods to get warm.  One time four does came running up to Gene and I and it was so cold that clouds of breath/steam was coming from their noses.  They were so close we could almost touch them with the end of the barrels of our 30-30 bolt-action rifles. 

We started hunting down near Neilsville, WI in unit 55, in Clark County off highway 10 and Columbia road.  At first, we would pull our mobile home down to the cemetery off Columbia road the night before.  Later, we moved our camping spot to the county park that was just up the road from the intersection of highway 10 and Columbia road.

Another of the memories about hunting in Unit 55 was the deer to deer hunter ratio.  Supposedly there were 30 deer per square mile and 30-35 hunters per square mile.  With sunrise came legal shooting time and an un-ending barrage of shooting that lasted at least two hours with never more than 30 seconds between shots.  For the first few years I recall one hunter who had some type of automatic rifle and would fire off a 5 second flurry of shots, only to disappear until the next season.  It was common to see blaze orange somewhere in sight and/or to have “deer pinball” going on as they ran from one hunter to another between shots.  The “biggest group” of deer I ever recall seeing down there at onetime was seven deer (all does) that ran by me one time.
