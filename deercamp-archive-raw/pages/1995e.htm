to the ridge.  Sometime around 8 a.m. a small doe came through traveling from left (east) to right (west).  Just before 10 a.m. another doe came by, this time moving from right (west) to left (east).  I watched it for sometime and then decided to take it, dropping the doe with one shot at 90 yards.  

Tuesday, November 21 - Cutting Up Venison

Given that we had three deer to cut skin and cut up, Gene and I decided to take Tuesday to get it all done.  We skinned and cut up the three does while Darlene and her mom Margaret cleaned up the hamburger meat and wrapped up the steak.  In all, it took nearly eight hours for the two of us to cut up the deer.  If it weren't for the beer, we would have had a hard time staying with the job.  Although I admit that as the day wore on and as beer after beer was consumed, we did become less picky about how much tallow was left on the venison that would be ground up into hamburger.  Since I normally only hunt until Wednesday each year, I decided to head back to Minneapolis after we were done with the deer.
