We decided to make the cedars drive since that is where Steve said the bigger deer went.  Chris, another of Arnie's son's and I would be the drivers.  The sign on this drive looked good with buck scrapes nearly under every low-hanging cedar branch.  I walked the tree line within sight of the field and Chris walked maybe 15 yards in.  We stayed close as the cover was thick.  Along the way I managed to kick up two turkeys which really surprised me.  At one point where there were lots of downed trees, Chris had to walk around the blow-downs.  As he did, he kicked up three deer, two does and a large buck with coal-black horns.  The deer ran out the side of the drive and appeared in front of Arnie who had set himself up on a known escape route.  Phil got off two shots at the buck but missed.  When he came out after the drive he explained what happened.  It was then that Steve knew that the larger deer he had seen in the morning was this buck.

But, with the sun in his eyes and the coal-black color of the buck's horns against the dirt-black background of the plowed field, he did not see this deer as a buck as well as the one  Eric shot.  We then at lunch and took all three deer to the registration station in Rock Falls.  We then drove back to shotgun hunting and planned our next drive.  This one was to be through another strip of woods that bordered the Chippewa River...only thing was the walk would be over one mile!
