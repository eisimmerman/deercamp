Eau Claire, WI

2000 - p.11.

Shot Gun & Rifle Country

November 26 – Day 9

Gene goes back to hunt near Colby and gets to sit on a stand.  Near the end of the day, 5 does come out to feed right in front of him.  Gene goes to pull the trigger on his 1968 Remington Model 742 autoloader and it jams.  All the while the deer feed right out in front of him.  We had this problem with this gun before, as it began jamming on me in the 1980's.  After having the gunsmith replace rusty parts in the action it was fixed.  Ten years later it looks like we have to do it again.  Gene closes out the season without seeing or shooting at any more deer.  

I recall only a few details around Dave’s successful hunt in taking a huge 8 pointer on the last day of the season.  A key comment from Duane stands out in my memory about where Dave should set his stand to try to intercept this big buck.  On Monday afternoon, day 3 of the hunt, Duane told Dave to move his stand to a certain spot where he had seen big buck sign.  Duane was certain the buck would be coming back around on his rut route and if Dave was there, he would catch the big buck coming back home.

December 9 – Day 1 Extended Deer Season

Back to Arnie’s for another day of rifle and shotgun deer action.  This time, Gene and Ben will meet us in Eau Claire as well.  Arnie, his two sons and myself go back to rifle country to do a drive.  I sit on a stand against a big tree that is next to a creek.  The Arnesons have taken quite a few deer from here, including the buck and doe on opening weekend.  Arnie’s older son Dennis pushes a big doe out that quickly jumps down from the ridge top to the bank of the creek and up and over the other side in what seems to be only seconds.  Just before the doe came out I had stood up to stretch.  Had I been sitting down, I might have been able to get a shot off through some branches, however the deer was only visible for a few seconds, so it is unlikely that would have worked anyway.
