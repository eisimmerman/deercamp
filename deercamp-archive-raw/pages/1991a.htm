Drummond Wisconsin

1991 -  p.2.

Year 2 Bayfield County

November 9, One Week to Opener

One week before the opener.  I leave Minneapolis at 6:30 a.m. and arrive at 10:30 a.m.  Appears to be at least two feet of snow on the ground.  I park at Johnnie’s corner and snowshoe to the shack, pulling my gear on a flat, roll-up toboggan.  Everything keeps falling off and I have to pick it up at least every ten yards.  I make at least five trips from the car to the shack.  Distance one way is about .6 mile.  Then, snow shoeing behind the shack takes time and I arrive at Gene’s stand around 1p.m.  Two and one-half hours just to get to the clearing behind the shack.  Setting up Gene’s ladder stand goes pretty quickly and I move on to my stand.  Hanging the tree stand goes pretty well too.  Deer have really been hitting the salt block under the pine tree.  Dirt is dug up in a circle four feet in diameter.  I find deer droppings under my tree that are 1.25” long.  According to Dr. Ken Nordberg's Whitetail Hunters Almanac, these would indicate a 4.5 year old buck, probably 8-12 points, a real trophy!  I manage to snowshoe out of the woods and back down porcupine to the car at Johnnies corner.  I feel ready to pass out.  So dehydrated I stop at the Triangle bar and down three tall glasses of ice water and two diet seven ups.  I leave at 4: 30 pm and get back to the cities at 8:30 p.m. 

Friday 11/15, Day Before Opener

Evening before the opener.  Lots of snow, the drive in is touch and go.  I make it to the shack and drive in and park when the snow stops the car.  Gene and Allen, in his 4Runner, are right behind me.  Paul is already there and has been out behind the shack looking for a stand.  Fred has been scouting near Clam Lake off highway 77.  Allen has found a new spot beyond Club Lake.  We all get a bit “buzzed up” and have a great time sharing hunting stories from prior hunting seasons.  We hit the rack about 10 p.m.
