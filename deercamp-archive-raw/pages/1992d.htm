Drummond Wisconsin

1992 - p.5.

Year 3 Bayfield County

Nov. 21  - The Opener

From day break to 8 a.m. I hear very few shots across to the mainland that I hoped would be the spot of most of the pressure needed to drive the deer off onto the swamp island.  Around 8 a.m. I hear a crunching noise that signals an animal is running my direction.  I push the safety off on the gun and wait.  As the sound gets louder I look harder to see something and finally identify a fox or coyote that is on a run toward the end of the swamp island, possibly going over to the mainland.

While getting my gear ready for this season I had bumped my scope pretty hard against something so I decided that I should try a shot to see that it is still “on”.  I follow the animal in the scope, pick a spot and shoot.  The animal drops instantly so I’m satisfied of my aim.  I continue to hunt until about 11 a.m. with little shooting around me and no deer seen.  Based on shooting that sounds like it is coming from closer to the hunting shack, I decide to get down and go back there for the afternoon hunt.  I go down the logging trail right behind the shack and go to Gene’s stand.  He hasn’t seen any deer either.  We agree that I can sit on his ladder stand and he will go back to the other ladder stand that is just off the clearing. 

The afternoon goes by slowly as given that there are only about 12 deer and 6 hunters per square mile, there isn’t a lot of movement typically.  Around 4 pm I see two deer out of my left perimeter vision.  They stop briefly and I throw up my rifle and put the scope on the chest of the closest one and fire.  They both take off and I fire three or four more times.  I notice that one of the deer is walking very slowly over the ridge down toward the creek.  I expect it won’t travel far.  Shortly, Gene comes over and we begin to track the deer.  There is a lot of blood and where the deer was walking slowly blood has really pooled up.  We continue tracking and find that both deer went down to the creek, jumped over and went up the other side.
