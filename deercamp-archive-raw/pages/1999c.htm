Abbotsford, WI

1999 - p.4

Year 6 - New Land

November 20  - The Opener 

The walk back in to the far stand was very slow and hard due to all the brush that was recently down on the ground from the clearing of the land to build a house on.  We finally get there and after tightening up the straps holding the tree stand, we begin to walk out on a course that will keep us on the land we are ok to hunt on.  Again, the going is very slow due to the thick brush.  At one point, I am trying to move some brush out of the way and find a stick that is stuck in the ground.  I begin bending it so that it will break and clear the trail.  However, before the stick breaks the end that is stuck in the ground comes out and hits me directly in my right eye!  The vision in my eye goes black and it feels like I have been in a boxing match.  I tell Gene I can’t see and feel my contact lens drop out.  I manage to catch it before it is lost on the ground.  We take a few moments to gather ourselves.  Gene looks at my eye and sees the redness and scratching from the stick, but otherwise says it looks ok.

I tell Gene that I still can’t see very well out of the eye and that I see black blobs around the perimeter of my vision.  On top of that, the eye is watering a lot and is hard to keep open.  So after a few more minutes, we complete our walk out to the logging trail.  We go back to Gene’s first stand and talk about what to do next.  By now it is after 11 a.m. and we decide to drag his buck out to my Toyota RAV4.  Because there is no snow, the drag is slow and hard.  We manage to get about 100 yards from the road we parked on, so I offer the idea that I should drive down to get the buck.  Gene agrees as we are both exhausted from the drag.
