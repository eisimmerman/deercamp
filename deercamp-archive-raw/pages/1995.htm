Abbotsford, WI

1995 - p.1.

Year 2 - Hunting Near the School Forest

Saturday November 11, Pre-Season Scouting

Because of the clear cutting that had been done in the school forest, Duane decides that he needed to find Gene and I new stands since the hunting would get crowded with less land and more hunters.  During bow season, Duane found good deer sign and some possible stand sites on land about four miles from the school forest.  The land was owned by his cousin Larry.

Gene, Dan (Duane's son), Duane and I walked in from the road to connect with a logging trail.  About 50 yards further in we come to a spot where two trails cross the logging trail so we pick out a tress to put my stand up.  The stand site looks southwest towards a fence line that the deer have been crossing.

A few hundred yards in further Duane and Dan find a good spot for Gene's ladder stand.  They place it in the middle of some tress that cover a fence line that separates Larry's land from his neighbors.  On the way out I put "bright eyes" tacks on the trees to mark the trail in to my stand.  Later that day Duane and Dan leave to go bow hunting.  Gene and I take some time to sight in our rifles by setting up a target down an abandoned,  straight logging road.  At dark I leave to go back to the twin cities and Gene goes home to Wausau.

Saturday, November 18 - The Opener

Gene and I wake up at Margaret's (Gene's mother-in-law) house in Colby at 4:30 a.m. so that we can be on stand and ready to hunt by 6:30 a.m.  It is a 30-minute drive from Colby to the school forest.  We park across from Larry's house and walk in.  There is about 3 inches of snow on the ground so it is a good thing that I put out the bright-eyes tacks to mark the trail since the woods looks very different when covered by snow.
