Eau Claire, WI

2000 - p.10.

Shot Gun & Rifle Country

November 22 – Day 5

Up at 5 a.m. to get to Arnie's house by 7 a.m.   The drive to Eau Claire from Minneapolis goes quickly.  I’m looking forward to this hunt as the last time I hunted here was in 1992 and I took a nice 5-point buck with a shotgun.  The land here is a mixture of farm fields and small woodlots so you can figure out where the deer are most of the time.  The hunting zones come together so that you can hunt in either shotgun or rifle country just by driving a few miles.  The mornings are usually spent on stand until around 9a.m. , and then we gear up for deer drives the rest of the day.

Checking in, I find Arnie and his family to be pretty much the same since last hunt in 1992.  So far this season, they have a buck and two does.  One son got the buck and doe on opening weekend and Arnie got the other doe later.  We talk about going over to rifle country for a few quick drives and then see what happens after that.  The first two drives pushed no deer so we case up and drive over to shotgun country.  I take out my dad’s old side-by-side 20 gauge shotgun and hope that if a shot presents itself that the gun can throw a slug straight enough to hit the deer.   One drive we make involves taking a boat from the mainland over to an island that is about 40 yards away.  This island has been an escape route for deer to hide in when they get pushed on the mainland.  Arnie and his son Jamie row across and do the drive with no deer seen.  For the rest of the day we make drives but see no deer until we are walking in to drive a section along the Chippewa or Eau Claire river.  

Arnie’s son Dennis and I were walking in to start the drive when we see four deer standing.  The deer take off and probably leave the section we are going to drive as nothing gets pushed out.  The last drive of the day we make results in deer also running out of the section of woods we want to drive before we have the standers set up.  So, we break at 4:30 p.m. as darkness falls.  I tell Arnie that if all goes well, I could either be back for a hunt on the last weekend or maybe for a day if they extend the season just for does in early December.
