Abbotsford, WI

1997 - p.2

Year 4

Saturday, November 22  – The Opener 

There is a light dusting of snow on the ground making it easier to see deer movement.  I recall one doe coming by within shooting distance, but since I shot two does the season before last year, I hold off for a buck.  In the afternoon a doe and a fawn bed down near me, but that is all the action that I see for the day.  

I think Duane set up Gene's stand near where Dave used to have his ladder stand, southwest from based camp, near the creek.  Dave had gained access to and 80-acre parcel of land directly north, across the road from the school forest, so he had moved his stand over there to hunt.  The morning was cold with wet blowing snow out of the southwest directly into Gene's face.  With snow ending late in the morning, we all gathered back at base camp for lunch.  As we were finishing up, we heard a deer drive starting in the area, so we all hurried back to our stands.  Gene especially thought he would be in a good position to intercept any of the deer that might be pushed by his stand.  

No sooner had Gene gotten back into his stand than he saw three does circling back into the school forest after being pushed out of the woods nearby.  Two of the three does ran off and one stayed back.  Gene placed the cross-hairs of the scope on an opening where the doe would walk and when its head and neck entered he fired, dropping the doe instantly.  No one else got any shooting the rest of the day.
