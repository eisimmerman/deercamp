Abbotsford, WI

2001 - p.9.

Opening Day

Saturday - November 17

I finally saw my first deer at about 9 a.m.  It was a doe that came from the South and ran in between Lorin and I and was gone just that quick.  Later in the morning, two two-man deer drives pushed through our hunting area, so it seems the local hunting gangs were trying to do all they could to disrupt our hunting.  Now that I was 20 feet off the ground and with a new seat that was just a "few inches too thick", I was only able to balance on my toes on the platform, so it felt just a bit unstable. 

So, at 11 a.m. I decided it was a good idea to take the last 4-foot extension section on my stand off and reset the stand to 16 feet.  Well as you might imagine, this was neither an easy nor quiet feat to pull off.  It seems that my prior work of putting up more straps to tie the stand down had worked well and it was very difficult to get the one section off the stand and out away from the tree with all the other tie-down straps on.  After about 30 minutes of struggling with this, I ended up keeping the 4-foot extension on the stand, but the only way I could get it back on, since I could not completely get it off, was to have it reversed where part of the brace which should go against the tree was facing outward and instead of fitting together nicely, it was just "resting" on the brace of the lower piece.  By now I was completely exhausted and sweated up, so I just climbed back up and sat down.  

At about noon, I got down as I was very thirsty and walked over to Lorin's stand to see if he had brought any water in.  He had not, so we decided to walk out to Mr. Truck to get a quick drink and light lunch.  We decide to get back into the woods and hunt in order to catch any of the "late lunchers" coming back in that may push deer our way.  I am back on my stand and ready to hunt by about 1:10 a.m.  Before I climbed into my stand, I set out three scent pads with deer lure in hopes of getting some activity around my stand.  About 30 minutes later I heard the sounds of deer running
