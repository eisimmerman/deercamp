We all walked back out to Gene's truck and drove back to the trailer to get lunch and for Eric to get the key to unlock the trigger.  After lunch we got back out into the woods and on our stands around 2 p.m.  Like the morning, the afternoon was beautiful with low wind, mild temperatures and sunny skies.  We all stayed on stand until about 6 p.m.  I had warned Gene that if I had not seen any does to shoot at I would take a shot at a tree around 6 p.m. to see where the rifle was shooting at.  Well, I did that and found out that it was shooting about 4 inches low at 40 yards, which would have been about 10 inches low at 100 yards.  So, I would have to fix that before the next morning.

For the night, we just spent it hanging around the trailer at the Pulaski Lake bar which was within walking distance.  Sleeping in the trailer was fun as the furnace was not working so that meant sleeping in 40 degree temps with lots of blankets.  

October 28 - Day 2

We managed to get out on the stand earlier today - 7:30 a.m. v. 8:00 a.m., but a funny thing happened on the way in to go to our stands.  Because we were running behind, Gene forgot something after locking his truck so he went back for it.  We were only a few yards down the logging road, so Gene turned around and went back to get it.  After finding it, Gene took a few steps back in towards the woods and instead of pressing the auto lock button on his remote, he mistakenly pushed the alarm key which set off the truck alarm that blasted for 10 seconds before he could get it turned off.  Even though we had just announced ourselves to every deer within a square mile, Ben and I thought that it was really quite funny.  So, we walked in and got on our stands and hunted only until 9 a.m.
