Abbotsford, WI

2001 - p.5.

Pre-season Scouting

November 10 - School Forest Area

Well, Gene volunteered to gut the buck out since, as he said, "it's been a while since I last did this" (knowing that Gene had did this when he shot his 8-point buck in 1999 and that I had not shot a deer since 1997, I graciously let the offer pass).  With expert coaching from Duane, Gene did a first-rate job.  Then comes the hard part, the drag out.  I offered to help Dan with this since the combination of no snow and thick brush would make it very hard for one man.  Luckily, Gene had brought a drag rope so Dan put that over his shoulder and I grabbed the buck's horns.  After many starts and stops (and falls) we managed to pull the buck out to the snowmobile trail.  Dan and I then walked down the trail to get this truck.  Dan backed it down the logging trail to within a few feet of where the buck laid.  

We all gathered back at the school forest to admire the buck.  Later on Duane weighs the buck on a scale at his house and it comes in field-dressed at 160 lbs., which would have put it over 200 lbs live weight.  By now the time was close to noon, so we agreed we needed to put up our tree stands.  Duane says I can go in and set up my tree stand by myself and that everyone else would go to the new land to put up Gene's and Ben's stands.  I add that I wanted Dan to come along to help place Lorin's stand somewhere in between our stand locations in 2000 because we noticed there was quite a few sets of tracks crossing in between that were out-of-sight for both of us.
