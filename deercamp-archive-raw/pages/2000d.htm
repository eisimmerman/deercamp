Year 7 - Larry Meyer's Land

November 18 – WI Deer Gun Opener

Nothing much happened until around 3 pm when deer movement picked up and shots starting ringing out with more frequency.  I had yet to see any more deer and at about 4:15 p.m. I heard a shot real close…must have been Lorin.  Then…a second shot 30 seconds later.  Great, I thought…Lorin must have dropped one on the first shot and fired the “coupe de grace” right after.

I quickly got down out of my tree and hustled over to Lorin’s stand.  As I got closer, I could see Lorin was standing on the ground and pointing.  I looked to see a deer down about 40-50 yards from his stand.  When Lorin came over he explained what had happened.  Just after 4 pm twin fork horn bucks came running toward him from the fence line out in front of him.   They stopped and looked at him so Lorin threw his gun up and took a shot at one of them.  After missing the shot, one of the bucks split off and the second one took off running straight at Lorin.  That led to the second shot at the other buck which entered the rib cage and dropped him about 40 yards from his stand.  If I recall, Lorin had his scope set up on high power (7?) so the first shot he only had a small field of view to shoot at. 

As Lorin began dressing out the buck, he told me about all the deer activity he had seen in the pm as well as a recap of the day.  For the day, he had seen between 11-13 deer with 3 of them being bucks.  The 3rd buck had snuck up on him earlier in the afternoon and when he turned around on the stand to look he saw the buck’s horns about the same time the buck saw him, so the buck whirled around and ran off before he could get a shot.  Considering everything, it was quite a day of hunting for Lorin, this opener being the first one for him in 23 years, since we hunted together in high school.
