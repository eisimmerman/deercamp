Day Trip Hunting

November 24 - Day 2

Sunrise came just before 7 a.m.  At 7:15 a.m. I remembered I had not yet used the deer call, so I got it out and "bleated" three times and then put it down.  At abut 7:20 a.m. I was looking to my left when I decided to look around the other direction.  When I slowly turned around I saw a deer feeding with its head down.  I then carefully turned around my seat to face the deer.  The seat was an upside down plastic pail with a cushion on top.  Fortunately, the floor of the box blind had carpet on it so it muffled most of the sound when I swiveled around.  But, it made just enough noise that the deer heard it and/or saw movement and threw up its head with its ears spread out to hear better.  A buck!  I quickly put the barrel of my rifle on the top rail of the box, centered the cross hairs on what I thought was the front shoulder and pulled the trigger.

I say I had the cross hairs on what I "thought" was the front shoulder because the light was still low and with the buck standing in brush I could not clearly see.  Sensing that the buck would not hang around much longer,  I shot and watched as the deer tried to run off back toward the field from where it had come.  It stumbled for about 10 yards and then fell into a grassy swale.  Yes!  The buck was down and I could hear/see it kicking out its last moments of life.  I looked at my watch and it was 7:25 a.m.  I got out of the box blind and paced off the distance to where the buck had dropped....about 40 yards. from my blind.  I then looked where the buck had been standing when I shot it and saw the kicked up leaves along the path where it had stumbled to where it dropped.  The killing single shot was made at about 50 yards.

As I reached down to grab the one horn that I saw to tell me it was a legal buck, I pulled the head up to find a fork on the other side.
