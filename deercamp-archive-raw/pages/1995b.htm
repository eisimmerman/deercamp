Abbotsford, WI

1995 - p.3.

Year 2 - Hunting Near the School Forest

Saturday, November 18 - The Opener

Well, I said the other hunter was my brother, that I was from Minneapolis and that Larry Meyer said we could hunt on his land.  As you could imagine, a local Wisconsin land owner really did not like to hear that someone from another state was hunting right next to him.  He gave me a hard time for not tagging my doe immediately after shooting it and before dressing it out. He said that his hunting party liked to let does go and only shoot at the bucks.  Finally the guy left and soon thereafter, Gene came walking up to me.  

Now Gene said that another hunter came up to him and said that he was Larry Meyer and told him to get off his land right away.  So Gene and I went back to his stand and took it down and drug it back to my stand.  Once we had mine down, we drug the doe out to the road and then went back in to get the tree stands.  So much for hunting on opening day.  Our only option was to go back to the school forest to meet Duane for lunch and let him know what happened.  

So we took the logging road off Cardinal and entered the school forest to park Gene's car.  At lunch time, Duane, Dan and two of their hunting crew - Dave and Joe, came back to base camp which was basically a turn around next to an old broken down picnic table.  Gene and I just hung out until everyone came back for lunch.  Duane could not believe that Larry had kicked us off his land.  He said it had to be someone from a local hunting party who was pretending to be Larry.  Duane said he would call Larry that night after hunting.  For now, Duane said he would help Gene go set up his tree stand nearby and put me on one of the other ladder stands that were already set up.
