Cornell Wisconsin

1974 -  p.3.

GeneM, Dad/Eric & Buzz

The hunting was mostly deer drives that went by names that described the geography or some memorable event or outcome from the drive. They were names like “hardwood drive” (behind Buzz’s farm a strip of hardwoods that bordered the corn fields and ended up in a pasture),  “mothers kitchen” (so called because it almost always produced venison for some lucky hunter), “old Moses” (a drive that Buzz shot this big old buck on, must have been a 14 pointer or better), “the wedge” (a small drive that was sandwiched in between two other plots of land), “Henning’s” (a drive behind the shack owned by a pair of bachelor brothers, Ted and Ben Henning).

I’m sure there were more.  I remember my Dad telling me that he had hunted deer since he was seven years old and had even shot a deer with a 22 rifle!  The other memorable thing about those deer drives; they seldom pushed out deer.  I only recall getting shots at one deer, a buck on a drive at mother’s kitchen.  Either Buzz or Big Gene put me in a field on a corner of a fence line that deer liked to come out of.  For some reason, I only put three shells in my 30-30-bolt action rifle.  Not having a fourth shell in the gun proved to be a big mistake.  Sure enough, a buck came out of the drive right at the corner on the fence line I was standing by.  After he jumped over the fence I shot at him three times as he was running away.  After my third and last shot the buck stopped in the middle of the field, turned around to look at me, then ran off again!

Well, we figured out what woodlot he ran into and planned our next drive.  Gene Melville and Buzz both got shooting at the buck and thought they had wounded him.  They were able to track him into another wood lot and circled around to see that he did not exit that lot.  So we were going to plan a walk through that lot to find the buck when Gene Simmerman said, “well he’s right there.”  Apparently the buck had just made it inside the edge of the wood lot and died there.
