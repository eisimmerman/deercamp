Minnesota and Wisconsin Deer Seasons

November 22 - The WI Opener

several deer come running into the scent pads from all directions.  So...here I sit with deer moving all around me without having a shell in my gun or ready to fire.  As it gets closer to first light, I decide to try to quickly put a shell in the chamber by pulling the action back so that if a buck is in the area I can get a shot at it.  Bad idea!  I pull the action back and as soon as I let it slide forward, the loud metallic sound scares the deer and they all run off.  

Well, I soon hear some shots around 7 a.m. but think they are farther off and not from anyone in our hunting party.  The morning starts off slowly and I have no other deer movement until 11 a.m.  I'm looking around and see four deer come in off the field.  Because of my low stand placement, the lead doe spots me as soon as she gets close enough.  I look over the other three deer and see that they all are does as well.  The lead doe looks directly at me and I hesitate to shoot as I would like to hunt for a buck.  After a minute, the lead doe turns around and heads back out to the field with the other three does following behind.  I watch as they make a detour around me out into the field and then enter the woods about 100 yards further down the logging trail.  Because of the slow morning and the fact that these four deer did not have a buck in the group, I decide I will shoot one of the does.  I see three of four does enter the woods from the field and try to time my shot so that I can hit the fourth deer.  Boom, I hit the doe and it drops.  It lets out a very load bawl so I shoot at it again.  I see the second shot hit an overhead branch so I take a third shot which connects and puts the deer away.

I get down and pace off the shot to the doe and it comes out at 100 yards.  I quickly take some pictures and then gut out the doe.  Because it dropped right on the logging trail, I just move it off to the side as later Phil will bring his truck around so we won't have to drag the deer far.
