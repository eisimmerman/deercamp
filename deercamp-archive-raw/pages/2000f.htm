Year 7 - Larry Meyer's Land

November 19, 2000 – Day 2

As is typical of the Sunday lunch, Duane brings out his portable gas grill to cook some fresh venison or “veniskin” as he likes to call it.  The venison is usually the tenderloin from a deer that someone has taken on Saturday.  Duane (and others) also like to fry up the deer hearts as well.  The other fun activity on Sunday at lunchtime is to tune in the Green Bay Packers football game to listen to over the truck speakers.  Almost everyone (except Dan the cowboy fan) is a diehard packers fan.  Everyone likes to give Dan a hard time about that!

So, here we have 9 guys standing around in blaze orange clothing, whooping it up when the packers make a big play or score, eating venison…. it doesn’t get any better than this!  If the packers are playing well, we might get back into the woods to hunt by 1:30 p.m.   If they are rocking’ we get back for the afternoon hunt around 2pm  

The land where most of the hunting gang hunts has changed greatly over the last few years.  We hunt near a combination of school forest and private parcels of land, which seem to be in constant state of logging so we go through cycles of dense forest to clear cutting to thick regrowth.
