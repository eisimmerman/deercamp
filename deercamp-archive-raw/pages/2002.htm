Elk Mound, WI

2002 - p1.

Day Trip Hunting

November 23 - Opening Day

This season was an unusual one from many perspectives.  First, Cathy was diagnosed with breast cancer, so the decision was made to not be away form home for he season but instead to drive over to Elk Mound, WI and hunt each day of the opening weekend with my friend Phil Arneson.  The trip over isn't too far or long, about 90 miles and 90 minutes.  Obviously, I did not have the opportunity to do any pre-season scouting either.

So, up at 3 a.m. and on the road by 3:30 a.m. so that I can get to Phil's farm at about 5:00 a.m.  I meet Phil at his house right at 5 a.m.  We drove our trucks over to rifle country in Eau Claire county.  Phil had me sit on the ground in a box blind, about 100 yards in off the field from where we park.  After he dropped me off I hung out 4 scent wicks and settled in for the hunt.  The first day was very slow.  I heard the first shot about 6:40 a.m.  Shooting around "rifle country" in Eau Claire county, about 5 miles from Phi's house, was light.  All shots I heard were in the distance and not from our hunting party.  While looking West, I did see a fawn run by at about 9 a.m.   Man, it was so small I thought it was a dog!  Everyone gathered at the trucks for lunch, with the report all around that the deer were not moving.  Temps were in the low 30's.  About mid-morning the winds picked up so with no snow on the ground it was almost impossible to see or hear anything in the woods.

Well, we ate lunch and then planned to do deer drives the rest of the day.  We made two drives in the same area in rifle; one through a stretch of hardwoods and the second through a band of thick cedar trees.  I was a stander on both drives but got no shooting as no deer were pushed out to anyone.
