Abbotsford, WI

1995 - p.2.

Year 2 - Hunting Near the School Forest

Saturday, November 18 - The Opener

We get to my stand just off the logging trail and Gene continues on toward his stand which is about 15 more minutes further in.  I am up on my stand and ready to hunt by 6:30 a.m. and as dawn breaks it really lights up the woods thanks to the snow cover.

Duane has told Gene and I to expect that the local landowners will probably sit on their stands nearby and then start making drives around 9 a.m.  There is some shooting nearby, but neither Gene or I see any deer movement until just before 9 a.m.  Sure enough, one of the local hunting parties makes a drive which pushes two deer in my direction.  I first hear and then see the two deer and see that they both are does.  They both first stopped out in front of my by the fence line, about 100 yards away.  They are stopped on the far side of the fence and wait until the drivers get closer.  

One of the does jumps the fence and starts running towards me and the other one runs along the fence line toward the road.  The doe that runs towards me continues on the trail that runs under a pine tree right out in front of me.  Before crossing the logging trail, the doe stops and looks around.  With a broadside shot at 50 yards, I put the crosshairs of my Simmons 2 x 7 scope on the front should and fire.  Amazingly, the doe crosses the logging road and runs off, circling around behind me and stops about 75 yards away in some thick pine trees.  I put the scope on it and fire again, this time it drops.

Getting down from my stand, I walk over to the doe.  After taking pictures, I field dress the doe.  While working on the deer, another hunter makes his way towards me.  Without identifying himself he begins to ask a lot of questions such as, "was the hunter back farther in with me and "where was I from" and " who said I could hunt on this land?"
