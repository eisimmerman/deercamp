Neilsville Wisconsin

1988

The Last Buck in Clark County

Our “big buck” of the opening deer season in Neilsville was a “nubbing buck” (buck fawn).  .  These deer got their name from the protruding “nubs” or beginning antler growth that sometimes had just pushed through their skull/hair.  It was Sunday morning and you could count on deer staying in the thicker cover after surviving the opening Saturday hunt.  I was hunting from Dad’s stand, looking west when I heard deer coming from behind me in the swamp that ran north – South.  I slowly turned around to see a couple of deer looking at me and after visually picking out the biggest one, put my scope on it and fired.  To my disbelief, the deer ran off out of sight.  I couldn’t believe it since it was only about 40 yards away and standing broadside to me. 

I then went to the spot where I shot at the deer and found some hair.  I followed the trail in the swamp where the deer ran off and after about 50 yards I found the deer.  As a tradition, any buck taken had its “horns” saved and mounted for the lucky hunter.  Out of all the deer horns that I threw away (since at one time we had moved them from one temporary storage site to the next as we moved), I managed to keep this one!
