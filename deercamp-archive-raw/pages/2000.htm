Abbotsford, WI

2000 - p.1.

Year 7 - Larry Meyer's Land

November 11, Pre-season Scouting

This year Lorin Toepper has rejoined our hunting party.  I have him meet me at Mr. B’s restaurant in Abbotsford , WI at 8:30 a.m. so we can drive to meet Gene, Ben, Duane and Dan at the hunting area.  I leave Minneapolis at 5:30 a.m. and get to Mr. B’s at 8:00 a.m.   I discover that since the highway was rerouted around downtown Abbotsford, that Mr. B’s has closed down.  The mall and other stores have closed as well.  Lorin arrives and we drive out to the Colby school forest.  Lorin and I arrive and to my surprise, Gene is right behind me!  Duane comes out of the woods from a morning bow hunt and introductions all around are made.

Gene tells Lorin and I that we will go set up our tree stands on Larry Meyer’s land, just a few miles away on Romadka road.  Duane’s son Dan leads us into the woods and first picks out a spot off a logging road that is close to where I shot a doe a few years ago.  The trails here look good.  We drop Lorin’s tree stand there and go back in further to find a spot for my stand.  Dan finds a spot somewhere close to where Gene had his stand and just as we are about to start setting up my stand, Dan spots another hunter’s stand about 50 yards away, so we move south and pick another spot

Dan takes off to go pick up Duane, who is helping Gene and Ben put up their stands on the 40-acre plot where Gene shot his buck in 1999.  My stand goes up quickly in about 20 minutes.  We then go back to Lorin’s stand and have some trouble getting it to stabilize against the tree.  We get the stabilizer bar up and then I suggest Lorin climb up the ladder stand, and while leaning in to the ladder go to the top and put a ratchet strap around the top.  While on his way up, the ladder begins to pull away from the tree and as I look up I see Lorin falling backwards while holding onto the ladder stand.  I duck and partially break his fall.
