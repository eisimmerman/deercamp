What is a Camp?

A simple idea for small groups.

A Camp is a private space shared by people who care about the same moments — family trips, seasons of life, shared experiences.

There are no followers, no likes, and no algorithms shaping what matters.

Camps are intentionally small, personal, and focused on memory — photos, voice, and context — not performance.

Think of it as a digital place that feels more like a journal than a feed.

Where DeerCamp Began

Before apps, we re-lived hunts the old way—stories, photos, and the racks on the wall.

DeerCamp started as a way to preserve the moments we’d otherwise forget—so camp stories never fade.

In our basement, we had a bar, a pool table, and walls lined with deer racks— each one a memory you could see. We called it the Deer Den. Walk downstairs and you could re-live a season without saying a word.

My dad could read the deer woods like a map. He knew where to place his stand, when to wait, and when to take the shot. He was also the best marksman I ever knew — steady, patient, and precise. His last DeerCamp was at age 52, gone now but always here in DeerCamp.

That wasn’t just a trophy on the wall. It was knowledge, experience, and time — captured in a single moment.
