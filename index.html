What is a Camp?

A simple idea for small groups.

A Camp is a private space shared by people who care about the same moments — family trips, seasons of life, shared experiences.

There are no followers, no likes, and no algorithms shaping what matters.

Camps are intentionally small, personal, and focused on memory — photos, voice, and context — not performance.

Think of it as a digital place that feels more like a journal than a feed.

Where DeerCamp Began

Before apps, before feeds — we had a place that made memories feel permanent.

The Deer Den was our “memory wall.” Every rack told a story. DeerCamp brings that feeling forward — private, personal, and built to last.
DeerCamp started as disposable cameras and printed calendars — then grew into a searchable archive spanning decades. Now it’s built for the next generation of camp stories.

Dad & the Trophy Deer

My dad could read the deer woods like a map.

He knew where to place his stand, when to wait, and when to take the shot. He was also the best marksman I ever knew — steady, patient, and precise. His last DeerCamp was at age 52, gone now but always here in DeerCamp.

That wasn’t just a trophy on the wall. It was knowledge, experience, and time — captured in a single moment.
